The Fractal

machines that call machines · elaboration runs outward

Four arithmetic strategies, and the core they all stand on. Counting lives at the right — the primitive act. Every circle to its left is a strategy that does not replace counting but calls it: COBO reaches into counting for its ones phase; Rearrange-to-Make-Bases and Chunking reach into COBO. Type a sum, pick a strategy, and watch the delegation happen: the token stops at a calling state, a pulse runs down the call line, the inner machine does the labor, and the result travels back as a single value the outer machine simply uses.

Previously: You discovered shortcuts — counting-on, COBO, RMB. Each one faster than the last.

Each strategy is a machine. But here is the thing — each machine uses the one before it. COBO does not just "know" addition. It calls the counting machine to do the adding. RMB calls COBO. Chunking calls COBO. Watch the little pulse: that is one machine asking another for help, and the number that rides back is the help arriving.

Notice what the outer machine's single step costs on the inside. The ledger keeps the honest count.

This architecture models the developmental prerequisite structure documented in CGI (Carpenter et al., 1999). Counting-on cannot exist without counting-all. COBO (bases-then-ones) cannot exist without counting-on. Each strategy is an FSM that invokes inner strategies as subroutines — the computational analogue of what Steffe calls "scheme coordination." One outer transition stands for many inner ones: the child who chunks is still counting, but the counting has been folded into structure.

Norton, A. (2008). Josh's operational conjectures. JRME, 39(4), 401-430. Steffe (1991). Norton and Steffe describe metamorphic accommodation as the most profound type of crisis-driven reorganization: driven by a "recursive and persistent perturbation" that projects operations from a lower plane of knowledge to a completely new, higher plane. The outward elaboration visible here is the geometric analogue — each new circle is a higher plane that presupposes the ones it calls. When the nesting exceeds what flat FSMs can represent, metamorphic accommodation is required: not just adding another circle, but changing what a circle is.

Each circle is a strategy automaton; each dashed line is an invokesCore relation — the outer machine's state transition delegates to the inner machine. This is the fractal architecture from Chapter 9 of the manuscript, and it is a picture of mediated action: the outer act does not discard the inner acts, it contains them — preserved, cancelled, and lifted into a single step (the ledger shows the exchange rate). The nesting follows the Chomsky hierarchy: counting is regular; COBO/RMB/Chunking are context-free — they need a call stack, and the stack panel on the right is exactly that stack, made public. The fraction crisis occurs at the context-sensitive boundary, where this kind of nesting breaks.

Negarestani, R. (2018). Intelligence and Spirit. Urbanomic/Sequence Press. Following Brandom's "laughing Santa" example, Negarestani describes finite-state machines as "automatic laughing bags" that recognize and produce regular languages. But "higher levels of syntactic and semantic complexity require more powerful abstract automata (context-free, context-sensitive, recursively enumerable)." The call structure shows this hierarchy in motion: each outer circle requires stack depth the inner circle does not. What the visualization cannot show is the boundary where nesting itself breaks — where the system would need to modify its own transition table. That is the context-sensitive boundary, and it is where fractions live.

Run the same sum four ways. Counting alone touches every single number — watch the ledger. Chunking touches three. The counting did not disappear: it moved inside. That is what a shortcut is — labor folded into structure.

You can see the calling. But can you feel it? What happens when you grab one of these machines and pull? The playground is next.

Diagnostic use: a student who "has" chunking but stalls mid-problem is often stalling inside a call — the outer scheme is intact and an inner scheme failed to return. The stack panel is the picture to hold in mind: ask which frame the student is in, not which strategy the student "is on."

The picture generalizes past arithmetic: an act is individuated by the acts it has internalized — the pulse and the returned value are the mediation, and the ledger is the sublation made quantitative.
