Three levels of units — what splitting requires

Recovering the whole from 1/4, four times, only takes two levels — and two levels stop at the whole's edge. The splitting operation — partitioning and iterating held together as one act — requires all three at once (Steffe & Olive, 2010; Hackenberg, 2010):

With two levels a child can exhaust the whole and name 4/4 — but 7/5 has no home: iterating past the whole destroys the whole being tracked. With the third level, 7/5 is unproblematic: seven iterations of a unit whose meaning stays anchored to a whole of five. That coordination — not repetition — is the splitting operation.
