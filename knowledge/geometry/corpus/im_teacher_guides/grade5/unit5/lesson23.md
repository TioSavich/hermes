# Divide Whole Numbers by Decimals
_Anchor ID: `IM-G5-U5-L23` — Grade grade5, Unit 5, Lesson 23_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Calculate quotients of a whole number divided by 0.2 or 0.02 and explain (orally and in writing) strategies used.
- Interpret (orally and in writing) diagrams and expressions that represent dividing a whole number by 0.2 or 0.02.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s divide whole numbers by decimals.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to divide a whole number by a decimal less than 1 by reasoning about the number of groups of one tenth or one hundredth in one whole.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to divide a whole number by a decimal less than 1 by reasoning about the
number of groups of one tenth or one hundredth in one whole.


Narrative
In the previous lesson, students divided whole numbers by one tenth and one hundredth and made generalizations
about how to divide any whole number by those units. In this lesson, students extend that work to divide whole
numbers by any number of tenths or hundredths (with total value less than 1). Consistent divisors are used in repetition
to highlight relationships between the dividends and the quotients (MP8). Students evaluate expressions with larger
divisors such as          in order to encourage them to use the relationship between multiplication and division. Rather
than drawing 12 unit squares and dividing all of them into groups of 2 tenths, students may draw a single whole divided
into 2 tenths and then use multiplication.
