# Relate Division and Multiplication
_Anchor ID: `IM-G5-U2-L6` — Grade grade5, Unit 2, Lesson 6_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Describe (orally) how equivalent multiplication and division expressions can represent the same diagram.
- Use a diagram to represent a whole-number division story problem with a rational solution.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s explore the relationship between multiplication and division.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to understand that dividing an amount into a whole number of equal parts can be interpreted as multiplying the same amount by a unit fraction.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to understand that dividing an amount into a whole number of equal parts
can be interpreted as multiplying the same amount by a unit fraction.


Narrative
In previous lessons, students interpreted a fraction as division of the numerator by the denominator, and equivalently,
as a whole number divided into equal sized pieces. In this lesson, students relate division of two whole numbers to
multiplying a whole number by a unit fraction. In the first activity, students are given an opportunity to solve a division
problem using any strategy and, in the Activity Synthesis, they examine how the solution can be interpreted in terms of
multiplication or division. In the second activity, students continue to explore the relationship between a fraction, a
division expression, and a multiplication expression.

In IM Grade 4, students multiplied a unit fraction by a whole number. In this lesson, students begin to explore how to
interpret a whole number multiplied by a unit fraction.
