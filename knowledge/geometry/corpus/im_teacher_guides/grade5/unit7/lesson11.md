# Patterns and Ordered Pairs
_Anchor ID: `IM-G5-U7-L11` — Grade grade5, Unit 7, Lesson 11_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Draw the graph of a set of coordinate pairs.
- Use additive rules to determine coordinates for points in the plane.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s represent patterns on the coordinate grid.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to represent corresponding terms in two patterns on the coordinate grid.

Narrative
The purpose of this lesson is to continue to analyze the relationship between two patterns by plotting corresponding
numbers on the coordinate grid. After generating patterns in previous lessons, students now generate patterns and
interpret corresponding terms as coordinates on the coordinate grid. They make these ordered pairs from the patterns
and plot them on the coordinate grid. They observe patterns and interpret the meaning of points on the coordinate grid
in terms of generating rules.

Advancing Student Thinking
If students only name that the number 10 is in the table for both rules, consider asking:
   • “What do you notice about the corresponding terms of the two patterns?”
  • “How can you use multiplication to show a relationship between the corresponding terms of the two patterns?”




Lesson Synthesis
“Today, we plotted points from two patterns on a coordinate grid and noticed patterns.”

Display the image from the student solution in the second activity.

“What does the graph tell you about the two rules?” (They both start at 0. That’s what the point    means. Then the
first rule has 2 and the second rule has 5.)

“How is looking at relationships between patterns in a table the same as looking at relationships between patterns on a
coordinate grid? How is it different?” (In the table, I can see each rule by going across or I can see the relationship
between rules by looking at columns. The points on the coordinate grid help me visualize how the two patterns are
changing relative to one another but they don’t help me see the pattern for each rule.)
