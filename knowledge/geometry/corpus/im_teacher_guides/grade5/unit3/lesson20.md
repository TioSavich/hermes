# Recipes and Fractions
_Anchor ID: `IM-G5-U3-L20` — Grade grade5, Unit 3, Lesson 20_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Ask (orally and in writing) mathematical questions about a situation involving fractions.
- Explain (orally and in writing) strategies for solving problems involving multiplication and division of fractions in a context.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s learn about how fractions relate to recipes.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to apply what they’ve learned about multiplying and dividing fractions to solve problems related to recipes.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to apply what they’ve learned about multiplying and dividing fractions to solve
problems related to recipes.


Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson provides
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4). In previous lessons, students
multiplied fractions and divided whole numbers and unit fractions. In this lesson, students work with the context of
recipes to apply what they’ve learned about multiplying fractions and dividing whole numbers and unit fractions.
Students learn about the type of information given in recipes and generate mathematical questions that could be asked
about a recipe. Then they solve a problem in which limited measuring tools are available to measure out the ingredients
of a recipe. Throughout the problem solving process, students have to identify relationships between the measuring
tools they have available and the amounts in the recipe.
