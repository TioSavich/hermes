# Fill the World's Largest Wagon
_Anchor ID: `IM-G5-U8-L8` — Grade grade5, Unit 8, Lesson 8_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally and in writing) strategies for solving multi-step volume problems in context.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s solve more problems about volume.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to solve problems involving volume. Students multiply and divide multi-digit whole numbers using the algorithms learned in the previous sections.

The purpose of this lesson is for students to solve problems involving volume. Students multiply and divide multi-digit

Narrative
In this lesson, students solve problems about filling the world’s largest toy wagon. In the previous lesson, students
estimated the volume of the wagon bed using information from an image. In this lesson, students are given the
dimensions of the wagon bed, and they calculate its volume as the first step in solving problems about how many bags
of sand and how many boxes of a given dimension it takes to fill the wagon. Students use what they have learned about
volume, the standard algorithm for multiplication, and partial quotients to solve these problems.

Activity 2                                                                                                     15 min

More Boxes

     Standards
Addressing     5.MD.C.5, 5.NBT.B.5, 5.NBT.B.6


The purpose of this activity is for students to solve another problem about the big red wagon using multiplication and
division. Instead of filling the wagon with sand, they consider filling the wagon with boxes and determine how
many boxes will fill the wagon. Unlike with the sand, the boxes do not fill the wagon completely and the number of
boxes that do fit is not a divisor of the total number of boxes. Accounting for these considerations will be the focus of
the Activity Synthesis. When students account for these constraints of the situation, they persevere in solving the
problem (MP1).
