# How Did You Add?
_Anchor ID: `IM-G1-U5-L2` — Grade grade1, Unit 5, Lesson 2_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) strategies for adding 2 two-digit numbers, without composing a ten.
- Match (orally) equations and base-ten drawings that represent the same addition strategies.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s add numbers and find matching equations.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add 2 two-digit numbers, without composing a ten, using methods based on place value and make sense of equations that represent addition methods.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add 2 two-digit numbers, without composing a ten, using methods based
on place value and make sense of equations that represent addition methods.


Narrative
In previous lessons, students added teen numbers and one-digit numbers. Students also added two-digit numbers and
multiples of 10 and two-digit numbers and one-digit numbers without composing a ten. In this lesson, students are
invited to add 2 two-digit numbers that are not multiples of 10 in a way that makes sense to them. Some students may
attempt to write equations, but are not required to do so. The teacher draws diagrams and writes equations that match
student thinking during the Activity Synthesis. Throughout the lesson, students explain their own methods, interpret
others' methods, and connect different methods (MP3). Students notice different ways to make use of the base-ten
system to count on or add by place (MP7).

Activity 3                                                                                                      15 min

Introduce Five in a Row—Add within 100 without Composing

     Standards
Addressing      1.NBT.C.4


The purpose of this activity is for students to learn Stage 5 of the Five in a Row center. Students add within 100, without
composing a ten. Partner A places a paper clip on the gameboard to cover one number in each gray row. They add the
numbers and place their color counter on the sum. Partner B moves one of the paper clips to a different number in the
same row, adds the numbers, and places their color counter on the sum. Students take turns until a player gets 5
counters in a row or the board is filled. Two gameboards are provided, one where students add a one-digit and a two-
digit number and one where they add a two-digit and a two-digit number. For this activity, students work with the
gameboard with one-digit numbers.

The gameboards will be used again in upcoming lessons. Consider copying on cardstock or laminating for future use.
