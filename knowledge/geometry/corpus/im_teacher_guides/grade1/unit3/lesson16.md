# Add 3 Numbers
_Anchor ID: `IM-G1-U3-L16` — Grade grade1, Unit 3, Lesson 16_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Determine (orally and using words or other representations) if an expression with three addends has the same value as an expression with 10 as an addend.
- Explain (orally) how to use a make a ten strategy to express an equation with three addends as an equation with two addends.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s add 3 numbers.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to apply the commutative and associative properties in order to make a ten when adding three numbers within 20, and make sense of equations with addition expressions on both sides of the equal sign.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to apply the commutative and associative properties in order to make a ten
when adding three numbers within 20, and make sense of equations with addition expressions on both sides of the
equal sign.


Narrative
In the previous lesson, students found the sum of three addends in which two addends made a ten. They considered
different methods for finding the sums. In this lesson, students make sense of equations with addition expressions on
both sides of the equal sign. One of the expressions has three addends, two of which make 10. Students apply what
they learned about the commutative and associative properties to match these expressions to a           expression, and
determine whether equations are true or false.
