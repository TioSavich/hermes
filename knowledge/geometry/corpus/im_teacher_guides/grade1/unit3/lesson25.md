# How Do You Want to Subtract?
_Anchor ID: `IM-G1-U3-L25` — Grade grade1, Unit 3, Lesson 25_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) strategies for solving Add To and Take From story problems within 20.
- Explain (orally) strategies for subtracting and how a strategy was chosen based on the numbers in the expression.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s use subtraction methods that work for the numbers in a problem.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to use subtraction methods flexibly to find differences.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to use subtraction methods flexibly to find differences.


Narrative
Students apply their learning from the unit to flexibly use methods to subtract within 20. There is not one correct
method to use, as it is based on each student’s understanding of the concepts of subtraction, their known facts, and the
numbers in the problem. It is helpful for students to listen to and make sense of others’ reasoning about which method
they used (MP3). In the first activity, students find differences in given expressions. In the second activity, students apply
subtraction methods as they solve story problems with the unknown in all positions.

Choose Your Own Subtraction Method

     Standards
Addressing      1.OA.C.5, 1.OA.C.6


The purpose of this activity is for students to find differences using methods they choose. As students work, they may
feel more comfortable with one method than another. The numbers were chosen to encourage different methods
(counting on and taking away), so students can consider the numbers in a specific expression as they find the difference.
During the Activity Synthesis, students share which method they used for a specific problem and why they chose it
(MP3).

problem can be solved using either addition or subtraction. Students may use either operation for the second and third
problems and any method that makes sense to them. If they write equations to match the stories, they should be able
to explain how the story and equation match (MP2).
