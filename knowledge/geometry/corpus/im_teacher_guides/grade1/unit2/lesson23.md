# Story Problem Information
_Anchor ID: `IM-G1-U2-L23` — Grade grade1, Unit 2, Lesson 23_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Determine (orally) if a story problem does not have enough information to solve the problem or if it has more than enough information to solve the problem.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s think about the information we need to solve story problems.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to analyze story problems that give too much or too little information.

Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4).

In previous lessons, students solved several different types of story problems. In this lesson, the story problems either
have extra, unnecessary information or not enough information to solve the problem. Students group the story
problems into one of these two categories. They then cross out the unnecessary information and solve the problems.
Students also see that sometimes a story can have extra information and also not have all of the necessary information
to solve the problem.

Card Sort: How Much Information?

     Standards                                                        Instructional Routines
Addressing     1.OA.A.1                                            • Card Sort


The purpose of this activity is for students to think about the information needed to solve different types of story
problems. In previous activities, students solved many different types of story problems given the necessary
information. In this activity, students sort story problems into two categories: those that do not include enough
information to solve the problem and those with some extra information given in the problem that is not needed to
solve the problem. This sorting task gives students opportunities to analyze story problems closely and make
connections (MP7). When students identify information they need to know and don’t need to know to answer a
question, they model with mathematics (MP4).


      Access for English Language Learners
      MLR8 Discussion Supports. Students should take turns finding a match and explaining their reasoning to their
      partner. Display the following sentence frame for all to see: “I noticed ___ , so I matched . . .” Encourage students to
      challenge each other when they disagree.
      Advances: Conversing, Speaking, Listening


      Access for Students with Disabilities
      Representation: Access for Perception. Students may benefit from hearing the story problems read aloud more
      than once.
      Supports accessibility for: Conceptual Processing, Language

Activity 2                                                                                                      20 min

Solve the Problems

     Standards
Addressing     1.OA.A.1


The purpose of this activity is for students to solve different types of story problems. In the previous activity, students
determined whether story problems did not have enough information or had unnecessary information in the story. In
this activity, students cross out any unnecessary information in the story problems that had too much information and
solve them. When students identify or find the information they need to solve a problem, they model with mathematics
(MP4).

As an extension to this activity, consider inviting students to ask questions to gather the information needed to solve the
problems with not enough information.

Here are sample responses to the information students need to gather:

  • Elena has 5 pencils.
  • Noah put 7 counters under his cup.
