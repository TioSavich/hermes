# Represent Story Problems
_Anchor ID: `IM-G1-U2-L1` — Grade grade1, Unit 2, Lesson 1_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Represent the actions of Add To and Take From, Result Unknown problems in different ways.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s make sense of story problems.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to represent and solve Add To and Take From, Result Unknown story problems in a way that makes sense to them.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to represent and solve Add To and Take From, Result Unknown story
problems in a way that makes sense to them.


Narrative
Students revisit these familiar problem types and the different ways the actions of the story can be represented. In the
process, students reveal what they understand about the operations of addition and subtraction. In these problem
types, the quantities can physically be joined or separated. This action leads to the resulting quantity. When students
connect their representations and what they understand about addition and subtraction back to the story problem and
explain the connections, they model with mathematics (MP4).

In the Lesson Synthesis, students share different ways they can represent a story problem. Collect student ideas on
chart paper and save the paper for reference in future lessons.

Act Out Stories

     Standards
Addressing      1.OA.A.1


The purpose of this activity is for students to make sense of the action in Add To and Take From stories by acting out the
stories. Students first listen to a numberless story problem and retell the actions. Then they act out the story with an
emphasis on actions that show joining or separating. As needed, invite different students to act out the same story to
ensure all students get an opportunity to participate and watch stories as they are acted out. In the Synthesis, students
make connections to what they did to act out the stories and the structure of each story problem.

      they did to act out the story and to connect their actions to the story.
