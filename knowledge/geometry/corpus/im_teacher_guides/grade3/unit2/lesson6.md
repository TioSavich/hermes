# Different Square Units (Part 1)
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U2-L6` — Grade grade3, Unit 2, Lesson 6_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Comprehend (in spoken and in written language) the meaning of the terms “square inch” and “square centimeter.”
- Use square inches and square centimeters to measure the area of a rectangle.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s learn about different units we can use to measure area.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to learn that there are different units that can be used to measure area, specifically the square centimeter and the square inch.

Activity 2                                                                                                      20 min

What’s the Area?

     Standards                                                        Instructional Routines
Addressing      3.MD.C.6                                          • MLR2 Collect and Display


The purpose of this activity is for students to estimate, and then find, the area of a square, using different standard units
of area (square inches and square centimeters). Students discuss why using square inches and square centimeters give
different measurements for the same area.

To measure the area of the square, students trace the square or cut it out, place the traced or cut square on the grids
from the previous activity, and determine the number of squares covered on each grid. The measurement in square
centimeters is not exactly 25 square centimeters because the square is 2 inches by 2 inches. The main idea is that it
takes more of a smaller unit than a larger unit to cover the same area. When students reference units in their area
measurements, they are attending to precision in language (MP6).

This activity uses MLR2 Collect and Display. Advances: conversing, reading, writing.
