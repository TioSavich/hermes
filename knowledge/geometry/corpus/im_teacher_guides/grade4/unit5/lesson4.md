# Solve Multiplicative Comparison Problems with Large Numbers
_Anchor ID: `IM-G4-U5-L4` — Grade grade4, Unit 5, Lesson 4_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Interpret (orally and in writing) diagrams that represent multiplicative comparison problems with greater quantities.
- Represent multiplicative comparison problems with equations and diagrams, and solve the problems.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s represent and solve multiplicative comparison problems, with greater numbers.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to extend their understanding of multiplicative comparison to situations that involve larger quantities and larger multipliers.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to extend their understanding of multiplicative comparison to situations that
involve larger quantities and larger multipliers.


Narrative
In previous lessons, students interpreted discrete diagrams in which each section represented one item and used them
to solve multiplicative comparison problems. They also represented multiplicative comparison situations in which
different parts of the problem were unknown.

In this lesson, students extend their understanding of multiplicative comparison, including ways to represent it, to
include comparisons with greater amounts and multipliers.

In the Warm-up, students notice that the discrete diagrams used in previous lessons become inefficient with greater
numbers. Later, they interpret tape diagrams, in which each section is labeled with a number, to represent a quantity
rather than one object. Students use these diagrams to determine the amounts being compared and the factor that
relates the amounts.
