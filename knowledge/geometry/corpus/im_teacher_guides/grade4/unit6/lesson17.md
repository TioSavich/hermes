# An Algorithm with Partial Quotients
_Anchor ID: `IM-G4-U6-L17` — Grade grade4, Unit 6, Lesson 17_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Comprehend (in spoken language) the meaning of the term “partial quotients.”
- Explain (orally and in writing) strategies for dividing three-digit numbers by one-digit numbers.
- Interpret (orally and in writing) recording methods for division that uses partial quotients.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s make sense of and use an algorithm that uses partial quotients.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is to introduce students to ways to record partial quotients when dividing multi-digit numbers.

The purpose of this lesson is to introduce students to ways to record partial quotients when dividing multi-digit
numbers.


Narrative
Previously, students have found quotients by decomposing a dividend and finding the quotient for each decomposed
part until all of the dividend is divided. Students also reasoned in terms of multiplication—adding partial products until
they reach the value of the dividend—and in terms of place value. They also used area diagrams and base-ten
diagrams—among other representations—to support their reasoning.

In this lesson, students use partial quotients and a couple of ways to record them systematically—by writing a series of
equations, and by using an algorithm that uses partial quotients.
