# How Much Is 10,000?
_Anchor ID: `IM-G4-U4-L6` — Grade grade4, Unit 4, Lesson 6_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) how to represent thousands and ten- thousands using base-ten blocks or diagrams.
- Represent 5-digit numbers with base-ten blocks and diagrams.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s represent 10,000.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is to develop a relative sense of ten-thousand and understand it as a unit consisting of 10 units of one-thousand.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is to develop a relative sense of ten-thousand and understand it as a unit consisting of 10
units of one-thousand.


Narrative
In this lesson, students build on their understanding of the base-ten structure to develop a sense of the magnitude of
10,000. They first use base-ten blocks and base-ten diagrams to build four-digit and five-digit numbers. They then use a
10-by-10 grid to represent 100 and work together to build a representation of 1,000, and then 10,000. Students may
notice the inherent multiplicative structure of the 10-by-10 grids or the array of 10,000 and use counting strategies to
identify significant groups of 10 (for example, 10 groups of 100 and 10 groups of 1,000).

What Is 10,000?

     Standards
Addressing      4.NBT.A.1


The purpose of this activity is to develop a sense of the magnitude of 10,000 and to establish ten-thousand as a unit
consisting of 10 units of one-thousand.

In the Launch, students learn that the 10-by-10 grid that represented 1 whole in a previous section now represents 100
in this activity. (It is important to establish that in these representations, each small square in the grid represents 1.)
Students begin by organizing grids of 100 into groups of 1,000. Some students may intuitively decide to group grids by
ten, while others may depend on counting each grid by 100. In the Activity Synthesis, students are invited to use their
grids to create a class chart to show 10,000 as 10 units of one-thousand.

Lesson Synthesis
“Today we used base-ten blocks, grids, and drawings to represent multi-digit numbers. We also used groups of
hundreds to build 10,000.”

“In first grade, we learned that 10 ones are in each unit of ten. In second grade, we learned that 10 tens are in each unit
of one hundred. If we count 10 units of a hundred, we have a thousand, which is a new unit.”

“Where in this class chart do you see ten of something making a new unit?” (Ten of the hundred grids make a row or a
unit of one thousand. Ten of the thousand rows make a unit of ten-thousand.)

“How might we represent a number like 13,000?” (Add three more rows of 1,000 to the chart.)

“What do you think the next unit will be after ten-thousands?” (hundred-thousands or millions)

“Ten groups of ten-thousand makes a new unit, hundred-thousand. We will learn about this unit in future lessons.”
