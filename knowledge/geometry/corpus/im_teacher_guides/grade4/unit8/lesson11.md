# Symmetry in Sports
_Anchor ID: `IM-G4-U8-L11` — Grade grade4, Unit 8, Lesson 11_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Present (orally and using other representations) the effects of removing symmetry from a sports field on its rules of play.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s use symmetry to think about sports.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to apply what they have learned about symmetry to the design of sports fields and rules of play.

Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4). In previous lessons, students
identified lines of symmetry in two-dimensional figures, identified figures with line symmetry, and drew figures with
different numbers of lines of symmetry. In this lesson, students apply what they have learned about symmetry to
consider how symmetry affects sports fields and courts. Students consider examples of baseball parks that are
symmetrical and asymmetrical. Then they consider how changing a sports field with a line of symmetry would affect the
game. In the last activity students design sports fields with no lines of symmetry and create rules for playing on the
newly designed field.

Activity 1                                                                                                       15 min

New Field

     Standards
Addressing     4.G.A.3


The purpose of this activity is for students to consider how a sport would change if it was played on a field or court that
wasn’t symmetrical. When considering how a lack of symmetry affects a sport, students need to decide to what degree
the field or court isn’t symmetrical and how the changes would affect playing the sport. When students model a
situation using a representation such as a drawing and think about the real-world implications of their model, they
model with mathematics (MP4). If students aren’t familiar with the field of play or the rules for a particular sport,
consider giving students access to books about sports or internet enabled devices for research about the sport they
choose.

students design their sports fields or courts, they make choices about how the field or court should be shaped and
consider the implications of their design when playing on the newly designed field or court. When students consider

Lesson Synthesis
“Today, we designed sports fields with no lines of symmetry and developed new rules for playing on the fields or courts
you designed.”

“What were some decisions you made about your design? How did the decisions you made affect the rules you created
for your design?” (We decided to make one side of the field shorter than the other side. This caused us to make a rule
about switching sides more often so the game would be fair. We decided to make one side of the field narrower, which
made it harder to score. We adjusted the scoring at the narrow side so scores were worth more points.)

Consider having students respond to the previous question as a journal prompt.
