# Count and Compare Collections
_Anchor ID: `IM-GK-U8-L2` — Grade kindergarten, Unit 8, Lesson 2_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Choose methods for counting and representing a group of objects.
- Compare and contrast (orally) methods for organizing and counting a group of objects.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s count and compare.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to count and compare collections of up to 20 objects.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to count and compare collections of up to 20 objects.


Narrative
This lesson is optional, because the standards do not require students to compare groups of objects or written numbers
beyond 10. As students represent and compare larger collections, organizing the objects and representations becomes
more important to help students keep track. Give students access to 10-frames to help them organize their collections if
they choose.

Collections can be created from classroom objects, such as connecting cubes, two-color counters, pattern blocks, or
buttons. Students can also bring in collections of objects to count from home.

If students need additional support with the concepts in this lesson, refer back to Unit 6, Section A in the curriculum
materials.
