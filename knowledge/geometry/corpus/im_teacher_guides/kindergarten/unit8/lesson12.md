# Make Dot Images
_Anchor ID: `IM-GK-U8-L12` — Grade kindergarten, Unit 8, Lesson 12_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Create a "How Many Do You See?" activity, using compositions and decompositions of numbers to 5.
- Explain (orally) strategies for identifying a total number of dots.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s make groups of dots.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to develop fluency with adding and subtracting within 5 as they identify compositions and decompositions of numbers to 5.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to develop fluency with adding and subtracting within 5 as they identify
compositions and decompositions of numbers to 5.


Narrative
Students participate in a How Many Do You See? routine and then create their own dot images to use in a version of the
routine in small groups. Understanding that numbers can be composed and decomposed in multiple ways is an
important step toward adding and subtracting fluently within 5. When they make dot images and share with their
partners, students practice fluency for arithmetic facts within 5, through visualization (MP7).

If students need additional support with the concepts in this lesson, refer back to Unit 5, Section A in the curriculum
materials.
