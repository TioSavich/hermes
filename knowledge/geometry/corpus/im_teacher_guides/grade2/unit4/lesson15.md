# Tall Enough to Ride
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U4-L15` — Grade grade2, Unit 4, Lesson 15_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Interpret a number line that represents a real-world context and explain (orally) how to use the number line diagram to make decisions.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s find out what amusement park rides people are tall enough to ride.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to use two-digit numbers that are represented on a number line to solve problems.

The purpose of this lesson is for students to use two-digit numbers that are represented on a number line to solve

Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4).

In previous lessons, students added, subtracted, and compared numbers within 100. They represented two-digit
numbers with a point on a number line. They also learned to measure length in standard units. In this lesson, students
use each of these skills to determine what rides at an amusement park different people are tall enough to ride. Given
height requirements, students use a number line to compare these requirements to the height of different people.
Students then plan a day at the park for a family based on the height requirements and other given constraints.

Lesson Synthesis
“Today a number line helped us figure out which amusement park rides some people are tall enough to ride. How was

and down instead of sideways.)
