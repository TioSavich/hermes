# Measure and Plot
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U9-L4` — Grade grade2, Unit 9, Lesson 4_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Create a line plot to represent measurement data and use line plots to ask and answer questions about measurement data.
- Use a ruler to measure the length of objects in centimeters and explain (orally) how to use mental strategies to solve length problems within 20.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s create line plots and add and subtract measurements of length.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add and subtract within 20. Students measure lengths of objects and represent measurement data in line plots. Then they answer questions about the data.

Activity 1                                                                                                      20 min

May I Sharpen My Pencil?

      Standards
Addressing      2.MD.A.1, 2.NBT.B.5, 2.OA.B.2


The purpose of this activity is for students to measure the length of objects (pencils) to the nearest centimeter and to
record their data in a table. Students add and subtract to answer questions about the data in the table and share
strategies for how they find sums and differences. The numbers in the chart were chosen to invite students to look for
ways to use methods based on the properties of operations and known sums within 20 to find the total lengths.


      Access for Students with Disabilities
      Action and Expression: Develop Expression and Communication. Give students access to base-ten blocks or
      connecting cubes to represent the numbers they will add. Encourage students to build a ten when they can.
      Supports accessibility for: Conceptual Processing, Organization


Required Materials
Materials to Gather
  • Pencils: Activity 1
  • Rulers (centimeters): Activity 1


Required Preparation
  • Each student needs an unsharpened pencil.
  • The activity works best if it is likely that students will have a range of pencil lengths between and among groups. If
    necessary, sharpen pencils to different lengths and distribute them randomly to students.
