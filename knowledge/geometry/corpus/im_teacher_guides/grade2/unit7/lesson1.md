# Compare, Count On, and Count Back
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U7-L1` — Grade grade2, Unit 7, Lesson 1_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Identify (orally and in writing) unknown numbers that complete a count by 10 or by 100.
- Interpret (orally) number line diagrams that represent strategies for adding and subtracting within 100.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s compare numbers and add or subtract.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add or subtract within 1,000, using number relationships.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add or subtract within 1,000, using number relationships.


Narrative
In a previous unit, students compared three-digit numbers, using a number line, and considered how close they were to
one another and their distance from zero. In this lesson, students compare numbers and use the number line to
consider ways to find the difference between 2 three-digit numbers. When locating numbers on the number line,
students recognize that when the numbers are relatively close, they can simply count on or count back to determine the
difference between the two numbers.

In the second activity, students analyze number lines and counting sequences that increase or decrease by 10 or by 100.
Using these number relationships, students label number lines by counting on or counting back by 10 or by 100. The
work of this activity helps prepare students to use and make sense of computation methods based on counting or
adding on by place, in upcoming lessons.
