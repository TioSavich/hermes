# Spending Money
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U6-L22` — Grade grade2, Unit 6, Lesson 22_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Determine (orally) the consequences of spending an amount of money in a real-world problem.
- Find the values of amounts of money in a real-world problem and present (using words and other representations) how much is spent, what it was spent on, and how much is left.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let's think about spending money.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to solve problems involving dollar amounts and to make decisions about spending money.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to solve problems involving dollar amounts and to make decisions about
spending money.


Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4).

In previous lessons, students found the values of groups of bills and solved one- and two-step word problems involving
money. In this lesson, students continue to solve problems involving dollar amounts in the context of spending money
at the fair. Students first look at how someone else spent their money and consider whether or not they should
continue to spend or save their money. Then students make decisions about how they would spend their own money.
They decide how much they would spend, what they would spend it on, and explain the reasoning for the decisions they
made.

The purpose of this activity is for students to plan how they might spend their own money at a fair. Students first
consider what amount of money makes sense to bring to a fair. They then look at price charts and record how they
would spend their money. They can decide whether they want to spend all of their money, or just some of it. Students
calculate how much they are spending and how much money they have left, as they make sure they have enough
money for everything they plan to buy. In the Lesson Synthesis, students discuss what influenced their decisions about
spending their money. When students consider assumptions about information not given in a situation and model a
situation, using a representation such as a drawing or equation, they model with mathematics (MP4).

Lesson Synthesis
“What decisions did you have to make as you thought about how you would spend your money? How did you make the
decisions?” (I had to decide if it was worth it to buy something or not. I had to decide if I had enough money for all the
stuff I wanted to buy. I had to decide if I should spend all of my money or if I should save some. I had to add up all the
stuff I wanted to buy to see if I had enough money. I had to think about what was the best decision to make.)
