# Let’s Make a Dollar
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U6-L17` — Grade grade2, Unit 6, Lesson 17_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Comprehend (in spoken and written language) that the term “dollar” refers to a unit of money with the same value as 100 cents and that the notation $ can be used to represent dollars.
- Find the value of a collection of coins that has a value greater than 100 cents.
- Represent an amount of money given as a dollar and some cents by drawing collections of bills and coins.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s make a dollar.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add and subtract within 100 to find values of 100 cents, and to recognize a dollar as a unit of money with the same value as 100 cents.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add and subtract within 100 to find values of 100 cents, and to recognize a
dollar as a unit of money with the same value as 100 cents.


Narrative
In previous lessons, students learned the values of quarters, dimes, nickels, and pennies. They used strategies for
adding within 100 to find the total value of a set of coins and found different combinations of coins that have the same
value.

In this lesson, students find different combinations of coins that have values of 100 cents. They learn that “dollar” is the
name of paper money (a “dollar bill”) and is also a unit of money that has a value of 100 cents. Thinking about 1 dollar
and 100 cents as the same amount connects to students understanding that a hundred is the same as 10 tens or 100
ones. They can use this understanding to support making a dollar out of dimes and other coin denominations.

Students count groups of coins that have values greater than 100 cents. They may count coins to make 100 cents, and
then count the value of the coins that are left over. Students record the value of the coins as “1 dollar and _____ cents.” In
grade 2, students will not write dollars and cents using decimal notation, as this notation is reserved for their study of
decimals and fractions in grade 4 and beyond.

The Value of a Dollar

     Standards
Addressing     2.MD.C.8, 2.NBT.B.5


The purpose of this activity is for students to find combinations of coins with values of 100 cents and to understand that
a dollar is a unit of money with the same value as 100 cents. They add within 100 by grouping like coins, using a ten, or
counting on. They find coin combinations that add up to 100 cents or one dollar and write the leftover amount as cents.
This work builds on students’ understanding of a hundred.
