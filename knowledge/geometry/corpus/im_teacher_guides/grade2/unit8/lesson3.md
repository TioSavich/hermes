# Is It Odd or Even?
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U8-L3` — Grade grade2, Unit 8, Lesson 3_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Comprehend (in written and spoken language) the meaning of the terms “even” and “odd.”
- Explain (orally) strategies for determining whether images show an even or odd number of objects.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s explain why the number of objects in a group is even or odd.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to determine whether representations of groups of objects show an even or odd number of objects.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to determine whether representations of groups of objects show an even or
odd number of objects.


Narrative
In a previous lesson, students learned the terms “even” and “odd” and saw that if a group has an even number of
objects, it can be separated into 2 equal groups and that each object can be paired with another object with no objects
left over.

In this lesson, students justify why a number is even or odd using methods based on making 2 equal groups, pairing
objects, or skip-counting by 2. Some students may begin to justify why a group of objects has an even or odd number of
members by using equations with 2 equal addends to represent even numbers of objects. In the second activity,
students interpret addition equations in this way and connect the equations to representations of 2 equal groups (MP2).
