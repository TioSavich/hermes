# Subtract Your Way
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U2-L5` — Grade grade2, Unit 2, Lesson 5_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) an approach for subtracting a one- digit number from a two-digit number.
- Interpret (orally and using other representations) subtraction strategies that involve decomposing a ten.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s subtract 1-digit numbers from 2-digit numbers.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to subtract a one-digit number from a two-digit number and describe decomposing a ten when subtracting by place.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to subtract a one-digit number from a two-digit number and describe
decomposing a ten when subtracting by place.


Narrative
In grade 1, students added within 100 using strategies based on place value and properties of operations. When using
place value strategies to add, students learned that sometimes they need to compose a ten. To this point, when
subtracting within 20, students used strategies like counting on, decomposing a number to get to a ten, and using
known addition facts, but they did not explicitly decompose a ten.

In this lesson, students subtract a one-digit number from a two-digit number when a ten would need to be decomposed
if they subtract by place. When subtracting a one-digit number from a two-digit number, students may count back to get
to a ten and then count back from there. Although subtracting or counting back in this way is an effective way to
subtract a single-digit number, it becomes less practical with greater numbers. For this reason, students are encouraged
to use cubes and base-ten blocks to help make decomposing a ten visible to all students and prepare students to
consider decomposing the minuend in order to subtract by place. For example,                  , so decomposing 26 into 1

How Do You Find the Value?

     Standards                                                     Instructional Routines
Addressing     2.NBT.B.5                                       • 5 Practices


The purpose of this activity is for students to subtract in a way that makes sense to them. Students use an approach of
their choice and share their approach with one another. This can serve as a formative assessment of how students think
about finding the value of a difference when subtracting by place and a ten must be decomposed. Although students
may use many ways to subtract, including those based on counting or compensation, the Synthesis focuses on
connecting these approaches to those based on place value where a ten is decomposed.

Monitor and select students with the following approaches to share in the Synthesis:

  • Show 82 with connecting cubes, remove 9 cubes, and count all or count back from 82 to find the difference.
  • Subtract 2 from 82 to get to a ten (80) and then subtract 7 from 80 by counting back (with or without connecting
    cubes or blocks).
  • Show 82 with base-ten blocks, decompose a ten to get 12 ones, remove 9 ones from 12 ones, and count the
    remaining blocks.
The approaches are sequenced from more concrete to more abstract as a way to invite all students to make sense of
subtraction approaches where a ten is explicitly decomposed. As an alternative, if the majority of the class uses the
same approach (including one not listed above), consider sequencing the approaches from more common to less
common (or less common to more common if the majority of students show decomposing with base-ten blocks). Aim to
elicit both key mathematical ideas and a variety of student voices, especially students who haven't shared recently.
