Adding pressure without taking over. Pressure becomes a craft. The teacher leans on the child's reasoning without taking it from them — a balance found in language, in real time, and earned again with each child.

The Grade 3 lessons show two different forms of geometry work.

  • In Using Square Inches to Measure, children use square units and half-triangle units to measure area.
  • In Rectangles and Rhombuses, children build and discuss quadrilaterals, including angles smaller and larger than right angles.

This discussion asks what kind of pressure a teacher should add without taking over the child’s reasoning.

Initial Post

Before reading classmates’ posts, choose one lesson.

Make a teacher-pressure artifact:

  1. Name the lesson.
  2. Name one child strategy you would want to preserve.
  3. Write one child sentence or written answer from that lesson.
  4. Say what you would refuse or press on.
  5. Write the teacher question you would ask next.
  6. Say what geometry idea the question is trying to make public: area unit, decomposition, half-unit, right angle, angle size, rectangle, rhombus, or quadrilateral attribute.

End with this sentence:

The pressure I would add is…

Aim for 250-350 words.

Peer Uptake Reply

Reply to your assigned response partner. If no partner is assigned, reply first to someone who chose the other lesson.

In your reply:

  1. Name one part of their pressure that keeps the child’s strategy alive.

  2. Name one part that might over-direct the child or leave too much hidden.

  3. Translate their teacher question into the other lesson.

  4. Add one sentence beginning:

    This makes me see the other lesson as…

Return Post

Return to your teacher-pressure artifact. Revise your question, defend it, or name the student evidence that would show it worked.

End with this sentence:

The teacher move became more precise when…

If no one has replied to you by the return-post window, use the reply you wrote to a classmate as your outside pressure and say so.

Where this points: Reasons you can check. The Grade 4 work raises the standard — child claims will now have to survive another child's audit, the first form of public justification at the child's scale.
