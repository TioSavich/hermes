Reasons you can check. Answer-getting becomes evident as the temptation it is. The Grade 4 work asks for reasons that survive a classmate's checking — the first form of public justification at the child's scale.

The Grade 4 lessons ask children to decide whether shapes are polygons and to find areas by decomposing shapes into rectangles and triangles. Both lessons can look like answer-getting, but both depend on reasons that can be checked.

Initial Post

Before reading classmates’ posts, choose one lesson.

Make a checkable-reason artifact:

  1. Name the lesson: Is it a Polygon? or Area of Polygons.
  2. Write one child claim from the lesson.
  3. Say what evidence would support the claim.
  4. Say what evidence would not be enough.
  5. Write one teacher question that asks for a reason without giving the answer.
  6. Name what the question protects: closed straight sides, line segments, parallel/perpendicular relations, decomposition, area units, rectangles, or triangles.

End with this sentence:

I would say no to an answer that…

Aim for 250-350 words.

Peer Uptake Reply

Reply to your assigned response partner. If no partner is assigned, reply first to someone who chose the other lesson.

In your reply:

  1. Name one part of their evidence standard you accept.

  2. Name one part you would make stricter or more flexible.

  3. Translate their evidence standard into the other lesson.

  4. Add one sentence beginning:

    The same teaching problem appears in both lessons when…

Return Post

Return to your evidence standard. Revise it, defend it, or name the borderline child answer that would test it.

End with this sentence:

My standard became more teachable when…

If no one has replied to you by the return-post window, use the reply you wrote to a classmate as your outside pressure and say so.

Where this points: Showing is not proving. Once children can check each other's reasons, the next prompt scales the same demand to the Pythagorean theorem — the deepest form of the first folding question is about to return.
