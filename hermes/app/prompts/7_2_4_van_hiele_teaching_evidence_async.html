Looking at how children look. The teacher steps back. Van Hiele levels are not labels for children — they are instruments for noticing what a child is treating as the object of thought. We are becoming observers of observing.

Materials

  • Geometric Thinking and Geometric Concepts

Van Hiele levels are not labels for children. They are tools for noticing what a child is treating as the object of thought: a whole shape, a property, a relation among properties, or a deductive system.

This prompt may make the levels sound cleaner than real children are. You may disagree with the prompt by naming a case where a child seems to move across levels.

Initial Post

Before reading classmates’ posts, make a level-evidence artifact.

  1. Choose one Van Hiele level from the reading.
  2. Write one child response to a geometry task that might show that level.
  3. Name the object of thought in the response.
  4. Name the product of thought in the response.
  5. Say what you would refuse if someone labeled the child only from whether the answer was correct.
  6. Write one teacher question that could move the child toward the next level.

End with this sentence:

I would place this response at this level only if…

Aim for 250-350 words.

Peer Uptake Reply

Reply to your assigned response partner. If no partner is assigned, reply first to a classmate who chose a different level.

In your reply:

  1. Name the evidence you accept for their level placement.

  2. Name one piece of evidence that is missing or ambiguous.

  3. Offer a teacher question or task change that would test the placement.

  4. Add one sentence beginning:

    I would not use the Van Hiele level as a label if…

Return Post

Return to your level placement. Revise it, defend it, or name the evidence that would decide the level.

End with this sentence:

The level became more useful for teaching when…

If no one has replied to you by the return-post window, use the reply you wrote to a classmate as your outside pressure and say so.

Where this points: Making structure public. Watching is not enough. The next prompt returns to Grade 5, where reasonable-looking answers hide their own structure, and the teacher's job is to bring that structure into language.
