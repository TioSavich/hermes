What changes, what stays. Transformations are where geometry meets identity through change. A shape moves; something is preserved. The invariants are the mathematics, and the command has to earn the picture it produces.

Materials

  • RoboCompass
  • RoboCompass

A transformation command can produce the right-looking image for the wrong reason. A good explanation names what changes, what stays invariant, and why the command sends shape A to the target shape.

For this discussion, use one of your assigned RoboCompass transformation problems.

Purpose

To practice connecting a command, a visual image, and the geometric meaning of a transformation.

Initial Post

Before reading classmates’ posts, make a transformation-evidence artifact.

Include your command and a picture if possible. Then answer:

  1. Which target shape did you construct from shape A?
  2. What command did you use?
  3. Name the transformation type: translation, rotation, reflection, dilation, or a composition.
  4. Name two things that stayed the same and one thing that changed.
  5. Explain how someone could check your command without trusting the picture alone.
  6. Name one way a different command might produce a similar-looking result.

End with this sentence:

My command really uses this transformation because…

Aim for 250-350 words.

Peer Uptake Reply

Reply to your assigned response partner. If no partner is assigned, reply first to a classmate who used a different transformation type.

Use your classmate’s command. In your reply:

  1. Name one invariant in their explanation that you can verify.

  2. Name one ambiguity in the command, center, line, vector, angle, or order of transformations.

  3. Suggest one label, coordinate check, or revised command that would make the transformation clearer.

  4. Add one sentence beginning:

    The image is not enough if…

Return Post

Return to your transformation-evidence artifact. Revise the command, defend it, or name the check that would settle the transformation.

End with this sentence:

My transformation explanation became more reliable when…

If no one has replied to you by the return-post window, use the reply you wrote to a classmate as your outside pressure and say so.

Requirements

Submit your initial post, your peer uptake reply, and your return post by the dates listed in Canvas. Include enough command information for a classmate to reconstruct your work.

Where this points: Carrying it forward. Invariants are how a thing remains itself across change. The last prompt asks the same of you — what your Grade 5 work has to become so that Grade 6 can be taught.
