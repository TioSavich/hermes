In a Euclidean construction, the drawing is not enough. The marks have to show that the shape was made using the allowed tools and that the required property follows from the construction.

For this discussion, use one assigned construction: an isosceles triangle that is not equilateral, a triangle with one side twice another, or a kite that is not a rhombus.

Purpose

To practice explaining why a ruler-and-compass construction works, not only showing what the final picture looks like.

Initial Post

Before reading classmates’ posts, make a construction-evidence artifact.

Include a photo or clear description of your construction, then answer:

  1. Which construction did you choose?
  2. What were you allowed to use? Name at least one thing you did not measure.
  3. List the main construction steps in order.
  4. Name the marks or relationships that show the required property is present.
  5. Name one part of your construction that could be wrong even if the picture looks right.
  6. Write one question you would ask a classmate to test whether your construction is valid.

End with this sentence:

My construction is valid only if…

Aim for 250-350 words plus your image or step list.

Peer Uptake Reply

Reply to your assigned response partner. If no partner is assigned, reply first to a classmate who did a different construction.

Do not only say whether the drawing looks good. Audit one step. In your reply:

  1. Name one step or mark from their construction you trust.

  2. Name one step where a hidden measurement, assumption, or missing compass mark might matter.

  3. Suggest one repair, label, or explanation that would make the construction easier to verify.

  4. Add one sentence beginning:

    The picture alone would not convince me because…

Return Post

Return to your construction. Revise one step, defend one step, or name the exact evidence still missing.

End with this sentence:

My construction became more than a drawing when…

If no one has replied to you by the return-post window, use the reply you wrote to a classmate as your outside pressure and say so.

Requirements

Submit your initial post, your peer uptake reply, and your return post by the dates listed in Canvas. Make sure your uploaded image shows compass marks or that your written steps name them clearly.

Where this points: When a shape becomes a tool. Once a construction can stand on its own, the next move is to use one shape as the instrument for building another. A rhombus is about to stop being a thing-to-recognize and start being a method.
