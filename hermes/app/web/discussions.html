Discussions

Give the app a classroom math discussion — pasted text, a photo of written work, a PDF or DOCX, or an audio recording — and it returns a plain-language report: every claim checked by the calculator, the conflicts between speakers, and where the class repaired a wrong turn. Pasted text is blinded to S01, S02… on this machine before any model call. A photo, document, or recording is first transcribed by the campus model (REALLMS), which reads the file itself; the transcript comes back here for your review, and the report passes it blind the same way.

Why this exists →

How to read a report. Two layers. Every mathematical claim is checked from the ground up and comes back holds, refuted, or not covered (outside current coverage — not the same as wrong). Then each notable move is described — settle a question, float a candidate, open an alternative, ask for an explanation — and how the class responded. The verdicts are computed; the readings of moves are interpretive.

What to look for. Conflicts (two speakers whose claims can't both stand) and repairs (a failed claim the discussion later returned to and got right).
