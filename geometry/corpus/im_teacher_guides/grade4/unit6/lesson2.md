# Patterns that Repeat
_Anchor ID: `IM-G4-U6-L2` — Grade grade4, Unit 6, Lesson 2_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Create a repeating shape pattern given a rule
- Describe (orally and in writing) features in a repeating shape pattern that were not explicit in a given rule.
- Explain (orally and in writing) strategies for predicting later terms in a repeating visual pattern.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s create and describe repeating patterns that follow a given rule.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to generate repeating shape patterns that follow given rules, explain apparent features that were not explicit in the rules, and use those features and what they know about properties of operations to predict future terms.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to generate repeating shape patterns that follow given rules, explain apparent
features that were not explicit in the rules, and use those features and what they know about properties of operations
to predict future terms.


Narrative
In an earlier lesson, students generated and described growing patterns that followed a given rule. They noticed that
there may be other features of a pattern that are predictable other than what’s stated in the rule. In this lesson,
students do the same with designs with shapes that repeat according to a rule. They continue to look for structure and
make use of it to identify and explain features of the patterns and to predict terms that may be inconvenient to find by
simply extending the pattern (MP7). For example, they represent each shape in the pattern with numbers and reason
about the repetition mathematically—by using operations and observing the properties of the numbers (MP2).
