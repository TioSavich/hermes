# Two Truths and a Lie
_Anchor ID: `IM-G4-U5-L18` — Grade grade4, Unit 5, Lesson 18_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Create (in writing) multiplicative comparison statements using an understanding of units of measurement.
- Justify (orally) whether a multiplicative comparison statement is true or false.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s compare facts about animals.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to apply their understanding of multiplicative comparison and measurement conversion to compare facts about animals.

This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide

both true and false statements involving multiplicative comparisons and unit conversions. They have an opportunity to
choose the animals to compare and which facts to use.

Then students determine which of their classmates’ statements are true and which are false. At the end of this activity,
they have an opportunity to revise their earlier statements to make them stronger and clearer.

Lesson Synthesis
“Today, we wrote some comparison statements and figured out if other groups' statements were true or false.”

“How did you decide what information to use as you wrote your comparison statements?” (I like dragonflies, so I
compared their speed to the speeds of other animals. I looked for measurements that could be compared with
multiplication, like how the bottlenose dolphin can live about twice as long as the southern elephant seal.)

Consider having students respond to the previous question as a journal prompt.
