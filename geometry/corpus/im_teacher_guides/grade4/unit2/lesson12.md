# Ways to Compare Fractions
_Anchor ID: `IM-G4-U2-L12` — Grade grade4, Unit 2, Lesson 12_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally and using words or other representations) strategies for comparing fractions.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s compare some fractions.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to compare fractions in a way that makes sense to them, including by reasoning about the size of fractional parts, common numerators or denominators, or relationships to benchmarks such as       and 1.

In previous lessons, students investigated the relative sizes of fractions with the same numerator or denominator. They

Some students may make comparisons by writing equivalent fractions, which shows they are applying learning from
earlier in the unit. However it is not necessary to highlight this approach at this point. In the next lesson, students will
take a closer look at how equivalence can be used to compare fractions.

are equivalent to      to classify and compare fractions. Along the way, students have opportunities to make new
observations about the structure of fractions that are less than                            , greater than   but less than 1, and greater than 1

students can code the fractions by putting circles, triangles, and squares around the fractions. In either case, a key or
legend should be created.

Lesson Synthesis
"In this lesson, we used three strategies to compare fractions.”

List the following strategies and read them aloud.

 1. If the denominators are the same, compare the numerators.

 2. If the numerators are the same, compare the denominators.

 3. If the numerators and denominators are both different, compare the fractions to benchmarks, like 0,                             , and 1.

“Which strategy do you think is most useful? Why?"
