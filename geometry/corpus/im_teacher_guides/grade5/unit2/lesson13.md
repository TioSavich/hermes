# Area and Properties of Operations
_Anchor ID: `IM-G5-U2-L13` — Grade grade5, Unit 2, Lesson 13_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Generate (in writing) expressions that represent the area of a rectangle with fractional side lengths greater than 1.
- Identify (orally and in writing) different numerical expressions that represent the same area of a rectangle with fractional side lengths greater than 1.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s write expressions to represent the area of rectangles.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to analyze area diagrams and use the properties of operations to represent the area of rectangles.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to analyze area diagrams and use the properties of operations to represent
the area of rectangles.


Narrative
In previous lessons, students applied what they know about multiplication of whole numbers and fractions to
decompose a rectangle to find its area.

In this lesson, students use the properties of operations to represent the area of rectangles with expressions. As
students go through the activities, they will apply concepts they have seen and used in earlier grades, units, and lessons,
such as:

  • The distributive property
  • The relationship between fractions and division
  • The relationship between multiplication and division
  • The relationship between addition and subtraction
Students use diagrams and expressions to investigate the distributive property, with both addition and subtraction. The
diagrams provide a way to visualize how the different expressions represent the area of a given figure. This generalizes
work that students have done in earlier grades with whole numbers. Understanding how the distributive property can
be represented with diagrams helps students understand the structure of expressions representing products (MP7)
and generalizes what they learned in an earlier grade using whole numbers (MP8).

Card Sort: Diagrams and Expressions

     Standards                                                        Instructional Routines
Addressing     5.NF.B.4                                           • Card Sort


The purpose of this activity is for students to analyze area diagrams and use the properties of operations to interpret
expressions. The diagrams are decomposed in different ways and the expressions all have a fractional part. On some
cards the fractional part is written as a mixed number and on other cards the whole number and fraction are separated
using the distributive property. This sorting task gives students opportunities to analyze diagrams and expressions
closely and make connections (MP7).

Advancing Student Thinking
If students only write one expression for a diagram, partner them with a student who found a different expression and
ask:
   • “How does this expression represent the shaded region?”
  • “How can two different expressions represent the same shaded region?”
  • “What other expressions can we write to represent the shaded region?”




Lesson Synthesis

more about?”
