Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to measure length and cut paper to a specified length.


Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4).

In previous lessons, students measured the length of objects by iterating same-size length units without gaps or
overlaps. They learned that the number of units they used is the length of the object. In this lesson, the class makes a
class quilt out of rectangular pieces of paper that they design and create borders for. Students begin by making
decisions about the size of the rectangles and drawing their own rectangle based on the decisions the class made. They
then determine how to make a border for the rectangle that is one cube longer on each side than the rectangle itself.
Students use any method that makes sense to them to make the border. Finally, all of the rectangles are put together
into a class quilt.

Patches for Our Quilt

     Standards
Building On     1.MD.A.2


The purpose of this activity is for students to make decisions about the side lengths of the patches they will make for the
class quilt. They decide whether or not all of the patches they make should be the same size, determine what the size
should be or what the restrictions on the size should be. They create the outline for their patch based on those
decisions. When students make assumptions about information not given in a situation, they model with mathematics
(MP4). Students then design the rectangle to represent one of their strengths or something they are proud of. Students
can create their designs using drawings and words. The rectangles will be used in the following activity to create a class
quilt.

Create a Border

     Standards
Addressing      1.MD.A.2


The purpose of this activity is for students to measure and cut paper to a designated length. This activity continues to
use the context of making a class quilt, as students decide how to create a border for the patch they created in the
previous activity. The directions are left open enough for students to choose different ways of creating their border.
When students consider assumptions about information not given in a situation, they model with mathematics (MP4).
Students discuss different ways this can be done, and finally put all the bordered patches together into a class quilt.

border for our patches, and used all of our patches to make a patchwork quilt. What was the hardest part of the quilt-

sizes. The hardest part was drawing the outline of my patch because I had to make sure it was the same length as 10
cubes.)
