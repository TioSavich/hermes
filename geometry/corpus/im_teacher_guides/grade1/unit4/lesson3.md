Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add and subtract 10 from multiples of 10.


Narrative
In this lesson, students build on their understanding of tens as they add 10 and subtract 10 from multiples of 10.
Students learn to think of adding or subtracting 10 as adding or subtracting a ten from a number of other tens. In the
first activity, students use towers of 10 to physically add or subtract a ten from a multiple of 10. In the second activity,
students learn a new stage of the Five in a Row center. Students choose a card with a multiple of 10 and decide whether
to add or subtract 10. The goal is to cover five spaces in a row on their gameboard. They can continue to use towers of
10 if helpful.

Activity 2                                                                                                   15 min

Introduce Five in a Row—Add or Subtract 10

     Standards
Addressing     1.NBT.C.4, 1.NBT.C.5


The purpose of this activity is for students to learn Stage 4 of the Five in a Row center. Students choose a card that
shows a multiple of 10 and decide to add or subtract 10 from the number on their card. They then cover the sum or
difference on the gameboard with a counter. If students can’t make a number that is left on the gameboard, they pick
another card. The game ends when a player gets 5 counters in a row or the board is filled.

The gameboard is provided as a blackline master. It will be used again in upcoming lessons. Consider copying it on
cardstock or laminating it for future use.


      Access for English Language Learners
      MLR8 Discussion Supports. Display sentence frames to encourage partner discussion during the game: “I will add
      10. The sum is _____.” and “I will subtract 10. The difference is _____.”
      Advances: Speaking, Conversing, Representing


      Access for Students with Disabilities
      Action and Expression: Internalize Executive Functions. To support working memory, provide students with access
      to sticky notes or mini whiteboards to keep track of solutions for adding 10 or subtracting 10 before making a
      choice of where to place the counter on the gameboard.
      Supports accessibility for: Memory, Organization
