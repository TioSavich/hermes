# Multiplication Strategies on Ungridded Rectangles
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U4-L11` — Grade grade3, Unit 4, Lesson 11_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Label ungridded rectangles and explain (orally) strategies based on properties of operations for calculating the area of each rectangle.
- Match (orally) expressions that represent the area of the same rectangle, including those that show the associative and distributive properties.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s use different strategies to find the area of ungridded rectangles.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to represent multiplication strategies on an ungridded rectangle.

properties. Here, they use the same strategies, but represent them on an area diagram without a grid. Then students
match expressions that could represent the area of the same rectangle, without using diagrams. The reasoning helps
students work toward fluent multiplication within 100.

Card Sort: Different Expressions, Same Rectangle

       Standards                                                    Instructional Routines
Addressing      3.MD.C.7.c, 3.OA.C.7                            • Card Sort


In this sorting activity, students identify expressions that could represent the area of the same rectangle and explain
their reasoning. To do so, students apply their understanding of properties of multiplication and draw rectangles as
needed in order to interpret parts of the expressions. Some students may sort expressions based only on their values.
Encourage those students to explain or show how they know, for instance, that             and                 can
represent the area of the same rectangle (MP7). Some of the expressions from this activity are used in the Synthesis to
highlight the commutative, distributive, and associative properties of multiplication.


       Access for Students with Disabilities
       Engagement: Develop Effort and Persistence. Chunk this task into more manageable parts. Give students a subset
       of the cards to start with and introduce the remaining cards once students have completed their initial set of
       matches.
       Supports accessibility for: Attention, Focus

Lesson Synthesis
“Today we matched expressions that could represent the area of the same rectangle. Let’s think about what some of the
matching expressions show us about multiplication.”

Display all the expression cards from the last activity.

“Which expressions show us that we can decompose one of the factors, then multiply them separately?” (E and K, C and
L, B and I)

Display the expressions on Cards F and G.

“What do these expressions show us about multiplication?” (When there are more than 2 factors, we can decide which
two factors to multiply first without changing the result. We can change the order or grouping of factors without
changing the product.)
