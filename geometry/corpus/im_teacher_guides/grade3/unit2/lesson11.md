# Area and the Multiplication Table
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U2-L11` — Grade grade3, Unit 2, Lesson 11_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Describe (orally and in writing) patterns in the products on a multiplication table, and justify why they happen.
- Describe (orally) the relationship between the area of a gridded rectangle and the representation of a product on the multiplication table.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s explore area and the multiplication table.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to explore connections between area and the multiplication table.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to explore connections between area and the multiplication table.


Narrative
This lesson introduces students to the multiplication table as a way to organize and find products of two whole-number
factors (up to 10). Students begin by marking rectangles on blank multiplication tables, always starting from the upper
left corner, and finding their areas. They see that the area of each rectangle is the product of the numbers at the right
boundary and the bottom boundary of the rectangle.

Through repeated reasoning, students see that finding the value of each cell in the table is like finding the area of a
rectangle whose side lengths are a number from the top of the table and a number from the left. Along the way,
students notice patterns in the table and make use of them to complete the rest of the table (MP8).
