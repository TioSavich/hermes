# Classroom Supplies
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U3-L21` — Grade grade3, Unit 3, Lesson 21_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Calculate the difference between quantities and describe (orally) comparisons, using “more” and “less.”
- Use and explain (orally) estimation or rounding strategies to solve a real-world problem.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s make a wish list.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to use their understanding of estimation, rounding, and addition within 1,000 to solve a problem about a class wish list.

The purpose of this lesson is for students to use their understanding of estimation, rounding, and addition within 1,000
to solve a problem about a class wish list.


Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4).

In this lesson, students put together a wish list of supplies they would like to get for their classroom given a large
collection of choices and their costs. They are given a budget and freedom to decide how to spend the money. As they
make choices, students round the costs before they check the total amount they are spending. Students then compare
their wish list with another group. Groups compare their wish lists and how much they spent in each category.

Activity 1                                                                                                    25 min

Make a Wish List

     Standards
Addressing     3.NBT.A.1, 3.NBT.A.2, 3.OA.D.8


The purpose of this activity is for students to make a wish list of items for the classroom with a $1,000 budget. As they
make their selections, they keep an estimate of the total by rounding, and use estimation and addition strategies to
remain within the budget. As students choose items for their wish list, they have more options than those shown in the
Warm-up.

Consider having students look up costs of additional items they would like to include in their wish list, using resources
such as websites (with help from an adult), book-fair flyers, or supply catalogs if available. When students consider
assumptions about information not given in a situation, model a situation, using a representation such as an equation,
and report on conclusions and reasoning, they model with mathematics (MP4).

      benefit from adding a third column to the supply lists that they can use to record rounded costs before they begin
      selecting items.
      Supports accessibility for: Organization, Conceptual Processing

What's on Your List?

     Standards
Addressing      3.NBT.A.2


The purpose of this activity is for students to present their selections from the previous activity to another group. They
explain their choices and compare how much money they plan to spend in each category. They make comparisons,
using “how much more?” and “how much less?” statements. When students report on conclusions and reasoning, they
model with mathematics (MP4).
