# Round and Round Again
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U3-L16` — Grade grade3, Unit 3, Lesson 16_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Describe (orally) a three-digit number including details based on rounding to the nearest ten or hundred.
- Determine (in writing) all the numbers that can round to a given multiple of 10 or multiple of 100, and describe (orally) observed patterns.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s look for patterns in rounding.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to use their understanding of rounding to consider all the numbers that round to a given number.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to use their understanding of rounding to consider all the numbers that round
to a given number.


Narrative
Students deepen their understanding of rounding to go beyond accurately rounding individual numbers as they think
about what numbers round to a given number. Working backward from a multiple of 10 or a multiple of 100 allows
students to think about the relative distances of numbers and the range of numbers that round to the given multiple of
10 or a multiple of 100. Students then use this understanding to write clues to help their classmates guess a mystery
number. The rounded number becomes a useful way to describe the mystery number in this game.

What’s My Mystery Number?

     Standards
Addressing     3.NBT.A.1


The purpose of this activity is for students to apply what they’ve learned about rounding to play a game in which each
student generates a mystery number with three clues. The three clues describe whether the mystery number is even or
odd, to which number it rounds, and two numbers that it’s between. It is possible that more than one number can fit the
clues provided. In the Activity Synthesis, students reflect on which clues were most helpful for determining the mystery
number.


      Access for Students with Disabilities
      Action and Expression: Internalize Executive Functions. Check for understanding by inviting students to rephrase
      directions in their own words. Keep a display of directions visible throughout the activity.
      Supports accessibility for: Memory, Organization
