# Tiny House: Design and Solve
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U8-L4` — Grade grade3, Unit 8, Lesson 4_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Ask (orally and in writing) and answer questions about the area and the perimeter of a rectangular floor plan partitioned into smaller areas.
- Present (in words and other representations) a design for a rectangular floor plan that follows given constraints.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s design a tiny house.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to design and solve problems about a tiny house.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to design and solve problems about a tiny house.


Narrative
In an earlier unit, students learned about area and perimeter. In this lesson, they apply their understanding of area and
perimeter, along with their creativity, to design a tiny house, which generally is a house under 400 square feet. After
students design their tiny house (along with the furniture and other details), they write, revise, and answer problems
involving area and perimeter that can be answered using their design.

If students need additional support with the concepts in this lesson, refer back to Unit 2, Section B in the curriculum
materials.

Lesson Synthesis
Invite a student to share their questions and ask their partner to share the answers.

If time permits, consider asking:

  • “What was something you noticed about the designs you saw today?” (A lot of the designs used rectangles to make
    the rooms. A lot of the bedrooms were the same size so they could fit a bed. Other students were really creative
    with how they used the space.)
  • “What was something you wondered about the designs you saw today?” (What do the designs made by people who
    build these houses look like? How do designers fit all the parts of a house into such a small space? Are there tiny
    houses that are a little bigger to include more things? Where can I learn more about building tiny houses?)
