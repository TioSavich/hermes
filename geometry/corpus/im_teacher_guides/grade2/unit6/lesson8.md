# Are All Pieces Created Equal?
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U6-L8` — Grade grade2, Unit 6, Lesson 8_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Draw and describe (orally) circles and rectangles that are partitioned into “halves,” “thirds,” and “fourths.”
- Generalize (orally) that equal-size pieces of the same whole can look different but remain the same size and have the same name.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s make halves, thirds, and fourths in different ways.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to understand that equal pieces of an identical whole do not need to be the same shape.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to understand that equal pieces of an identical whole do not need to be the
same shape.


Narrative
In previous lessons, students learned that when they partition circles and rectangles into halves, thirds, or fourths, each
piece must be equal. To this point, they have determined whether the pieces are equal by cutting out pieces and
physically matching them and by visually inspecting whether the pieces appear to be the same equal-size shapes.

In this lesson, students learn that halves, thirds, and fourths of the same whole can be different shapes or have different
attributes. For example, they recognize that a square is partitioned into fourths, whether it is partitioned into equal-size
triangles or equal-size squares.
