# Identify Pennies, Nickels, and Dimes
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U6-L15` — Grade grade2, Unit 6, Lesson 15_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Comprehend (in spoken and written language) that the terms “penny,” “nickel,” and “dime” refer to coins with values in cents.
- Comprehend that the term “cents” refers to a unit of money and that the notation ¢ can be used to represent cents.
- Identify (orally and in writing) the names of coins and explain (orally) a strategy for finding the value of a collection of coins.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s learn about coins and their values.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to identify pennies, nickels, and dimes, and to find the total value of a set of coins.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to identify pennies, nickels, and dimes, and to find the total value of a set of
coins.


Narrative
In previous units, students learned to use a ten when adding or subtracting within 100. They also used counting on or
counting back as a strategy when adding or subtracting, including counting on or counting back by 5 and by 10.

In this lesson, students recognize pennies, nickels, and dimes, and learn their values. Students find the value of a set of
coins by adding within 100. They also may skip-count or count on to find the value of a set of coins. When finding total
values of a mixed combination of coins, students may group like coins, use multiples of 10 to add within 100, or count
on. Students use the cent symbol (¢) when writing total values of coin collections.

Students first identify each coin and confirm they have assigned the correct values to the coins. They need to know the
