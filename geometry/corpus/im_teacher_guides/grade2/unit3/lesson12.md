# Saree Silk Stories: Friendship Bracelets
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U3-L12` — Grade grade2, Unit 3, Lesson 12_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) strategies for representing and solving two-step Take From and Compare problems about length.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s solve 2-step story problems about length.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to represent and solve two-step story problems. Students use representations to make sense of problems, support their calculations, and explain their thinking.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to represent and solve two-step story problems. Students use representations
to make sense of problems, support their calculations, and explain their thinking.


Narrative
In previous lessons, students represented and solved one- and two-step story problems in a way that made sense to
them. They have used tape diagrams to represent comparisons and part-part-whole relationships.

Since students are familiar with centimeters and inches, the stories in this lesson include both. In the first activity,
students make sense of and solve a two-step problem where both parts are Take From, Result Unknown. In the second
activity, they work together to make sense of two-step problems that include more challenging problem types, including
two-step problems that might require using two different operations.

Advancing Student Thinking
If students represent and solve the first part of each problem accurately, but clearly show they have not yet completed
both steps or identified the answer to the problem, consider asking:
   • “What do you need to figure out? What do you already know?”
  • “What happened in the first part of the story? What did you figure out? What else do you need to do to solve the
    problem?”




Lesson Synthesis
“Today, you solved different kinds of story problems that had two parts.”

“How did you represent your thinking and keep track of your calculations? How did you keep up with the lengths you
knew and what you needed to find out?” (I used diagrams to make sense of the story. I drew base-ten diagrams to help
me solve the problem. I put a circle around my answer so I could use it for the next problem.)

“What ideas for solving story problems have you learned from others?”
