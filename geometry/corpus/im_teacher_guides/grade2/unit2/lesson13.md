# Story Problems and Equations
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U2-L13` — Grade grade2, Unit 2, Lesson 13_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Match (orally) story problems and equations that represent the same quantities and relationships.
- Represent and solve Add To and Put Together story problems.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s make sense of equations and solve story problems.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to make sense of equations and connect them to the quantities in different types of story problems. Students solve story problems of different problem types within 100 that require composing or decomposing a ten.

Card Sort: Story Problems and Equations

     Standards                                                       Instructional Routines
Addressing     2.NBT.B.5, 2.OA.A.1                               • Card Sort


The purpose of this activity is for students to connect story problems to the equations that represent them and to solve
different types of story problems. Students identify equations with a symbol for the unknown that match a story
problem and justify their decisions by describing how the equations represent the quantities and any actions in the
story problem (MP4). When students analyze and connect the quantities and structures in the story problems and
equations, they are thinking abstractly and quantitatively (MP2).

      they disagree.
      Advances: Conversing, Representing

Represent and Solve Story Problems

     Standards
Addressing      2.NBT.B.5, 2.OA.A.1


The purpose of this activity is for students to use tape diagrams and equations to represent different types of story
problems within 100. In this activity, students interpret story problems and use diagrams and equations to represent
the unknown quantities. Students are encouraged to solve using a method that makes sense to them.

Students may complete the parts of each problem in an order that makes sense to them. In the Activity Synthesis,
students compare and connect their diagrams, equations, and methods for solving (MP2, MP7). Monitor for students
who draw accurate diagrams and create different equations for the problem with Noah’s and Kiran’s seeds to share in
the Lesson Synthesis.
