# Design a Garden
_Anchor ID: `IM-GK-U6-L13` — Grade kindergarten, Unit 6, Lesson 13_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Create or draw a collection that represents a real- world context.
- Write numbers to represent a quantity.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s make a vegetable garden.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to draw groups of up to 20 images in the context of arranging vegetables in a garden.

The purpose of this lesson is for students to draw groups of up to 20 images in the context of arranging vegetables in a

Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4).

In previous lessons, students counted and counted out groups of up to 20 objects. They also counted and drew out
groups of up to 20 images. In this lesson, students practice counting out objects and drawing images as they design a
garden and determine how many and what types of vegetables they will plant. As students make a model of the garden,
they may use what they have learned to organize the vegetables. For example, they may arrange the vegetables in rows
of 5 or 10.

If there is additional time, students can participate in centers from the unit.

Design Your Own Garden

     Standards
Addressing      K.CC.B.5


The purpose of this activity is for students to count out groups of objects and draw out groups of images in the context
of planning a garden. Students decide which types of vegetables to include in their garden, how many of each vegetable,
and how to arrange the vegetables. When students model a situation using a representation such as a drawing and
consider assumptions about information not given in a situation, they model with mathematics (MP4).
