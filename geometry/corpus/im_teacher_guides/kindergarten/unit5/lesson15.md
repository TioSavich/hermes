# Packing Up Fruit
_Anchor ID: `IM-GK-U5-L15` — Grade kindergarten, Unit 5, Lesson 15_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Present (orally and using other representations) compositions and decompositions within 10 that represents a solution to a real-world context.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s pack snack boxes.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to compose and decompose numbers to 10 and represent situations involving compositions and decompositions of numbers to 10.

The purpose of this lesson is for students to compose and decompose numbers to 10 and represent situations involving

Narrative
This lesson is optional because it does not address any new mathematical content standards. This lesson does provide
students with an opportunity to apply precursor skills of mathematical modeling (MP4).

In previous lessons, students composed and decomposed numbers to 10, both in the context of story problems and
using math tools. In this lesson, students choose how to pack a box with two different types of fruit. Then they write an
expression to represent how many of each fruit is packed in the box.

If there is additional time, students can participate in centers from the unit.

Lesson Synthesis
“How did you choose to show how you would pack the fruit in the box? Did you use objects or draw a picture or do
something else?” (I used connecting cubes. I drew a picture.)

“If you used objects, how did the objects help you show how you would pack the box?” (I used objects, because I could
show the bananas using yellow connecting cubes and the apples using green connecting cubes.)

“If you drew a picture, how did drawing a picture help you show how you would pack the box?” (I drew a picture, so I
could draw each fruit and write how many of each fruit would be in the box.)

“If you did something else, how did it help you show how you would pack the box?”
