# Equivalent Fractions
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U5-L10` — Grade grade3, Unit 5, Lesson 10_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Comprehend (in spoken and written language) the meaning of the term “equivalent fractions.”
- Explain (orally) how to determine if diagrams represent equivalent fractions.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s identify equivalent fractions.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to see that different fractions can be equivalent if they are the same size of the same-size whole.

The purpose of this lesson is for students to see that different fractions can be equivalent if they are the same size of

Narrative
Previously, students were introduced to unit fractions and non-unit fractions through the use of area diagrams, fraction
strips, and number lines. They began to work with the idea of equivalence by noticing fractions that are also whole
numbers. Here students revisit area diagrams and fraction strips to learn about fraction equivalence. Students learn
that fractions that are the same size are equivalent fractions. Later, they will identify equivalent fractions as having the
same location on a number line.
