# Tiny House: Cost
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U8-L5` — Grade grade3, Unit 8, Lesson 5_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Present (in words and other representations) strategies for solving real-world, multi-step addition word problems.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s calculate the cost of finishing a room in a tiny house.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to apply what they’ve learned about operations to calculate the cost to finish a space in a tiny house.

The purpose of this lesson is for students to apply what they’ve learned about operations to calculate the cost to finish a

Narrative
In a previous lesson, students used what they learned about area and perimeter to design a tiny house. In this lesson,
they apply their knowledge of operations to calculate the cost of finishing one room of their tiny house. Students engage
in aspects of mathematical modeling as they make decisions about quantities, relate measurements and costs, and
interpret their results in context (MP4).

If students need additional support with the concepts in this lesson, refer back to Unit 3, Section A in the curriculum
