# Multiply Numbers Greater than 20
_Anchor ID: `IM-G3-U4-L16` — Grade grade3, Unit 4, Lesson 16_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) strategies for multiplying within 100, where one factor is greater than 20, including strategies based on place value and properties of operations.
- Interpret (orally) base-ten diagrams and area diagrams that represent multiplication within 100, where one factor is greater than 20.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s multiply numbers that are greater than 20.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to multiply within 100, where one factor is greater than 20.

Previously, students have used strategies based on place value and properties of operations to find products of a one-
digit number and a teen number. Here, students extend this work to greater two-digit numbers. Students first analyze
different strategies and then apply one or more of them to find the value of other products. The lesson also includes an
optional game students can play to apply what they’ve learned to multiply greater numbers.

Play Close to 100, Multiplication

     Standards
Addressing           3.OA.B.5
Building Toward      3.OA.C.7


The purpose of this activity is for students to play a game in which they are able to apply the strategies they’ve learned
for multiplying one-digit numbers by two-digit numbers. Students use digits to create an expression that has a value as
close to 100 as possible. The first game includes teen numbers, and the second game includes two-digit numbers
greater than 20.

This activity is optional because it provides extra practice for multiplying by two-digit factors. Depending on the time
available, students can play 1 or 2 games.
