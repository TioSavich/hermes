# Groups up to 110
_Anchor ID: `IM-G1-U6-L8` — Grade grade1, Unit 6, Lesson 8_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) strategies for organizing and counting length units when measuring a length up to 110 length units.
- Match (orally) base-ten diagrams and base-ten numerals that represent the same value.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s measure lengths that are longer than 100 cubes.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is to measure length and count and read numbers to 110.

Narrative
In previous units, students counted quantities and read and wrote numbers up to 99. In previous lessons of this unit,

using centimeter cubes and represent their work in a way that makes sense to them. In the second activity, students
make sense of representations of larger numbers. In the Lesson Synthesis, students learn that 10 tens is 100.

Long Lengths with Small Cubes

     Standards
Addressing     1.MD.A.2, 1.NBT.A.1


The purpose of this activity is to measure a length that is over 100 length units long and count the number of units using
grouping methods. Some students may count by 1, others may organize their cubes into groups of 10. Although some
students may attempt to represent their counts with a written number, the emphasis is representing the length of the
string with a drawing. Students will identify written numbers in the next activity and practice writing them in the next
lesson.

     Standards                                                       Instructional Routines
Addressing     1.NBT.A.1                                          • Card Sort


The purpose of this activity is for students to match representations of tens and ones to written numbers up to 100.
Students may count by tens and ones. Some students may see that 10 tens can be grouped to make 100. After they
count, students match the written number to the picture. This matching task gives students opportunities to analyze
base-ten diagrams and two- and three-digit numbers closely and make connections (MP2, MP7).
