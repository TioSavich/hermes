# Write Equations for Story Problems
_Anchor ID: `IM-G1-U6-L15` — Grade grade1, Unit 6, Lesson 15_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Present (using words or other representations) a strategy for solving addition and subtraction story problems with unknowns in all positions.
- Represent a strategy for solving an addition or subtraction problem with an equation.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s solve story problems and write equations.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to solve story problems with unknowns in all positions using addition and subtraction. Students write equations to represent each story problem.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to solve story problems with unknowns in all positions using addition and
subtraction. Students write equations to represent each story problem.


Narrative
In the previous lesson, students matched story problems to equations.

In this lesson, students work in pairs to solve four different types of story problems and show their thinking using
drawings, numbers, words, or equations. Students create visual representations of their thinking for one story problem
and participate in a Gallery Walk in which they write equations to match others’ representations and show how they
made sense of one another's work. Throughout the lesson, students make connections between story problems,
representations, and equations (MP2).

Prior to this lesson, the teacher may consider reading Sadako and the Thousand Paper Cranes, by Eleanor Coer to the
class.
