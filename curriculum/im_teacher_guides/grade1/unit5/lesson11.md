# How Did You Do That?
_Anchor ID: `IM-G1-U5-L11` — Grade grade1, Unit 5, Lesson 11_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Interpret (orally) base-ten diagrams and equations that represent strategies for adding 2 two-digit numbers by place.
- Present (using words and other representations) strategies for adding 2 two-digit numbers.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s add 2-digit numbers and make sense of equations.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to add 2 two-digit numbers, with composing a ten, using methods based on place value and properties of operation and make sense of equations that represent addition methods.

It's Your Turn to Add

     Standards
Addressing      1.NBT.C.4


The purpose of this activity is for students to find the sum of 2 two-digit numbers using one of the methods students
have seen in previous activities. Students choose which method they would like to use and show their thinking using
drawings, numbers or words. Although students have been analyzing base-ten drawings, if they are ready to represent
their addition methods with equations, they should be encouraged to do so. Students participate in a Gallery Walk in
which they observe the different ways the addition methods can be represented.


      Access for English Language Learners
      MLR7 Compare and Connect. Synthesis: After the Gallery Walk, lead a discussion comparing, contrasting, and
      connecting the different posters. To amplify student language, and illustrate connections, follow along and point
      to the relevant parts of the displays as students speak.
      Advances: Representing, Conversing
