# Drawings to Represent Story Problems
_Anchor ID: `IM-GK-U4-L11` — Grade kindergarten, Unit 4, Lesson 11_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Present (using other representations) a drawing for representing a Take From, Result Unknown problem.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s draw a picture of a story problem.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to draw a picture to represent and solve a story problem.

Activity 2                                                                                                   15 min

Picture Gallery Walk

     Standards
Addressing      K.OA.A.2


The purpose of this activity is for students to compare drawings to represent story problems. Students participate in a
Gallery Walk to see the different drawings that were created. Consider creating a cue or signal to indicate when students
should move from one drawing to the next. In the Activity Synthesis, students compare and make connections between
using detailed pictures and symbols such as circles, to represent the story. Students also share different ways to
represent subtraction in a drawing (MP2).


      Access for English Language Learners
      MLR8 Discussion Supports. During the Gallery Walk, invite students to pair verbal explanations with pointing to
      the corresponding parts of the drawings when answering the question, “How does your drawing show what
      happens in the story?”
      Advances: Speaking, Representing
