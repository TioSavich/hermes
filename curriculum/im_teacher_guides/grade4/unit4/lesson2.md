# Equivalent Decimals
_Anchor ID: `IM-G4-U4-L2` — Grade grade4, Unit 4, Lesson 2_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Determine (orally) whether 2 ways of expressing tenths and hundredths are equivalent.
- Match (orally) diagrams and decimals that represent the same value.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s think about equivalent decimals.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to reason about equivalent tenths and hundredths in decimal notation.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to reason about equivalent tenths and hundredths in decimal notation.


Narrative
Previously, students learned to represent tenths and hundredths shaded on a grid as fractions and in decimal notation.
They continue to build their understanding of decimals in this lesson and take a closer look at decimals that are
equivalent (for example, 0.2 and 0.20). Students articulate why the same value can be expressed in two different ways.
They also encounter decimals in equations and on number lines, and use these representations to reason about
equivalence.
