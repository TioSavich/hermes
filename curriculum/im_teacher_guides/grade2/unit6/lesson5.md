# Center Day 1
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U6-L5` — Grade grade2, Unit 6, Lesson 5_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) how to draw a given shape by describing its attributes.
- Identify (orally) a shape, based on given attributes. Ask questions to elicit the attributes needed to identify the shape.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s work with shapes.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to practice describing, naming, and drawing triangles, quadrilaterals, and hexagons, based on their defining attributes.

This lesson is optional because it is an opportunity for extra practice that not all classes may need. In Activity 1, students

Activity 2                                                                                                  20 min

Introduce Can You Draw It—Grade 2 Shapes

     Standards
Addressing     2.G.A.1


The purpose of this activity is for students to learn Stage 2 of the Can You Draw It? center. Students describe and draw
two-dimensional shapes. One student picks a shape card, without showing their partner. They describe the shape
to their partner, who draws it based on the description. Together they compare the shape that was drawn to the shape
on the card. If the student has drawn the shape correctly, they keep the card. The partner with the most cards at the
end of the game wins. Students work with triangles, quadrilaterals, and hexagons. Before playing, remove any cards that
do not show a triangle, a quadrilateral, or a hexagon.
