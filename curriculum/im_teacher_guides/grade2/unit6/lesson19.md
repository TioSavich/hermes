# More Money Problems
_Anchor ID: `IM-G2-U6-L19` — Grade grade2, Unit 6, Lesson 19_

## Learning Goals (teacher-facing)
- Explain (orally) strategies for solving two-step Put Together/Take Apart and Compare problems.
- Match (orally) diagrams and story problems, including two-step problems.

## Student-Facing Learning Goals
Let’s solve money problems with a lot of dollars.

## Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to solve addition and subtraction story problems within 100, in the context of money.

Lesson Purpose
The purpose of this lesson is for students to solve addition and subtraction story problems within 100, in the context of
money.


Narrative
In previous lessons, students found the values of sets of coins and solved story problems with cents. Students learned
that a dollar is the same as 100 cents.

In this lesson, students revisit the more challenging story problem types in the context of money. This includes Compare
problems and two-step problems, where the steps are not explicitly stated. In the first activity, students revisit the use of
a tape diagram to make sense of problems and match tape diagrams to story problems. In the second activity, students
are invited to solve the problems in the way that makes sense to them. Students recognize that money problems are
solved the same as other story problems, but the cent and dollar symbols are included in the answer.

stories while the other pair are two-step stories. The numbers in the stories are the same, so students will have to focus
on the relationships between the quantities in the stories to match them to tapes (MP2).
