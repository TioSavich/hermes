   A recipe for fizzy juice says, “Mix 5 cups of cranberry juice with 2 cups of soda water.”

   To double this recipe, we would use 10 cups of cranberry juice with 4 cups of soda water.
   To triple this recipe, we would use 15 cups of cranberry juice with 6 cups of soda water.

   This diagram shows a single batch of the recipe, a double batch, and a triple batch:




   We say that the ratios        ,    , and        are equivalent. Even though the amounts of
   each ingredient within a single, double, or triple batch are not the same, they would make
   fizzy juice that tastes the same.
