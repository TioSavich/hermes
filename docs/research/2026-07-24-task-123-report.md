# Task 123 report: curriculum-dynamics archaeology and Hermes precedence

Date: 2026-07-24

## Finding

The recalled work is not one curriculum-precedence computation. It combines
three related artifacts:

1. The paper's heat-map figure is an iteration-5 **coverage carpet over
   arithmetic fact cells**. It compares which hand-written strategy templates
   apply at level 1 and level 2, then counts the applicable level-2 templates.
   It does not traverse lessons or a curriculum graph
   (`../umedcta-formalization/docs/proposals/2026-07-10-curriculum-dynamics/curriculum-dynamics.tex:496-520`,
   Fig. `carving-reachability`).
2. Iteration 13 is the **inference-budget reachability computation**. It finds
   minimum-cost paths through a small authored state graph whose nodes are
   `(accumulated value, current unit)` and whose stage gate admits `iterate`,
   `regroup`, or `partition` moves. It does not contain lessons
   (`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/units_machine.pl:1-25,38-69`).
3. Iterations 14 and 15 provide the closest thing to a curriculum surface: an
   **Atlas of one-step lesson-task transitions by entering stage**. Its cells
   come from source-backed lesson task instances, but every transition begins
   with an empty strategy inventory. It does not compose lessons in published
   order and does not compute lesson prerequisites
   (`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration14/generate_cells.pl:1-8,23-26,55-66`;
   `../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration14/export_atlas.pl:29-37,94-121,133-158`).

The transfer verdict is **right-tree-wrong-branch**. Budgeted reachability and
stage-conditioned path comparison are useful shapes for Hermes after a
provenance-bearing dependency relation exists. The heat-map carpet, the unit
machine, and the Atlas do not supply that relation.

## 1. The heat-map: generator, inputs, outputs, and exact semantics

### Generation chain

Iteration 5 defines 104 SLURM cells: a 24-experiment basic suite and an
80-experiment tolerance sweep. Each cell runs one named carving experiment and
writes clauses, pairs, residue, and statistics; the aggregator invokes the
recovery audit and the two plotting programs
(`../umedcta-formalization/exploratory/bigred/iteration5/README.md:10-27,44-59`).
`generate_experiments.py` obtains the experiment names from
`tools/carving/run.pl` and writes `cells.json`
(`../umedcta-formalization/exploratory/bigred/iteration5/generate_experiments.py:22-37,40-60`).
`aggregate.py` reads the per-cell CSVs and runs `tools/carving/analyze.py` to
produce `parametric_surface.png`
(`../umedcta-formalization/exploratory/bigred/iteration5/aggregate.py:1-14,81-103`).
The paper image
`docs/proposals/2026-07-10-curriculum-dynamics/carving-addition-reachability.png`
is a crop of the addition panels in that full surface
(`../umedcta-formalization/docs/proposals/2026-07-10-curriculum-dynamics/curriculum-dynamics.tex:510-521`,
Fig. `carving-reachability`).

### Input representation

The input is not a curriculum map. It is a bounded table of arithmetic facts
`fact(Operation, X, Y, Z)` plus a hand-written library of
`prove(Fact, Config, Proof)` templates. The templates were authored from the
strategy automata, mathematics-education literature, and the project's
vocabulary; the project explicitly classifies the result as a coverage survey,
not strategy discovery
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/CARVING_CLAIM.md:5-18,24-38`).

For the exact addition panels, both runs use base 10, operands through 20, a
single-digit seed, and tolerance 5. The only relevant configuration change is
`unit_levels: 1` versus `unit_levels: 2`
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/run.pl:220-237`). The seed is an
authored recollection table populated before carving
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/run.pl:22-34,108-125`). The level-2
opening is also authored in the rule clauses: counting is bounded by
`Config.tolerance`, while RMB and decimal decomposition require
`Config.unit_levels >= 2`
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/primitives.pl:40-49,84-103,105-134`).

The experiment output is:

- `*_clauses.pl`: one clause per successful fact-template proof;
- `*_residue.txt`: facts for which no template applied;
- `*_stats.txt`: configuration, elapsed time, and aggregate counts;
- `*_pairs.csv`: one row per `(operation, x, y, z, strategy)` plus explicit
  `RESIDUE` rows
  (`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/run.pl:127-198`).

The local iteration-5 results show the exact contrast used in the figure. At
tolerance 5, level 1 carved 271 of 441 addition facts and left 170 residue
cells; level 2 carved all 441 and left no residue
(`../umedcta-formalization/exploratory/bigred/iteration5/work/outputs/base10_level1_seeded_stats.txt:1-7`;
`../umedcta-formalization/exploratory/bigred/iteration5/work/outputs/base10_level2_seeded_stats.txt:1-7`).

### Exact cell semantics

All three panels use `x` as the first addition operand and `y` as the second,
with both axes covering integers 0 through 20. The plotting code stores a cell
at `grid[y, x]` and gives the plot the labels `x` and `y`
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/analyze.py:201-239,311-349`).

- **Upper-left panel, addition L1:** a cell color is the display-priority
  strategy selected from the applicable level-1 templates. Black
  (`RESIDUE`) means the arithmetic fact exists in the bounded input table but
  no admitted template derived it. The panel is therefore a categorical
  template-coverage map, not a scalar cost map
  (`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/analyze.py:38-55,111-134,201-239,853-859`).
- **Upper-right panel, addition L2:** the axes and categorical cell semantics
  are unchanged. The admitted template set now includes level-2 rules, so the
  prior residue is covered by templates such as RMB and decimal decomposition
  (`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/analyze.py:823-830`;
  `../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/primitives.pl:84-134`).
- **Lower panel, addition L2 entitlement plenum:** each cell value is the
  number of distinct non-`RESIDUE` strategy names applicable to that fact. The
  color bar encodes that integer. The displayed crop reports an average of 3.0
  and a maximum of 6 proofs per fact
  (`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/analyze.py:242-258,363-383,882-885`;
  `../umedcta-formalization/docs/proposals/2026-07-10-curriculum-dynamics/carving-addition-reachability.png`,
  lower panel).

The fixed tolerance of 5 is a homogeneous-step bound inside certain proof
templates, not the plotted cell value and not a curriculum inference budget.
Iteration 5 did produce a separate tolerance-sweep figure whose horizontal axis
is tolerance and whose vertical axis is percent of bare facts carved
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/primitives.pl:10-14`;
`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/analyze_sweep.py:1-13,89-116`).

### Where the gates came from

The level and tolerance gates were authored as conditions on the hand-written
proof templates. They were not derived from standards, curriculum ordering, or
observed learner paths. The project's own claim file says the visible patterns
are the interaction of the authored parity, divisibility, base-distance, and
memoization gates, and that the predictions are not independent evidence
because the project wrote both the gates and the predictions
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/CARVING_CLAIM.md:24-38,60-65`).
The fraction L3 rules make the same provenance explicit: the
`unit_coords >= 3` threshold is a configuration gate, distinct from the
whole-number `unit_levels` parameter
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/primitives.pl:712-725,732-778`).

There are consequently no prerequisite edges in the heat-map computation.
There are proof-body steps within a template and conditions that admit a
template, but neither relation says that one lesson must precede another.

## 2. The budgeted reachability computation

Iteration 13 is the artifact that actually matches the remembered
inference-budget language. Its graph has state `u(Value, Unit)`, begins at
`u(0,1)`, and uses four authored move types. `iterate` adds the current unit;
stage 2 admits `regroup` and `back_to_one`; stage 3 admits `partition`. Each move
costs one, and breadth-first search returns the minimum cost and a witness path
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/units_machine.pl:38-69,71-98`).
The unit inventory and stage thresholds are encoded directly in the machine,
including composite groups 2 through 12 and bases 100 and 1000
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/units_machine.pl:40-55`).

The searched objects are whole-number and fraction targets, not curriculum
nodes. `export_annealing.pl` emits one JSONL record per target and stage with
`reached`, `min_cost`, strategy signature, and witness. Whole targets cover
1 through 500; fraction targets use denominators 2 through 12
(`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration13/export_annealing.pl:1-25,37-54,92-109,125-175`).

The inference budget is applied after the search. The Python aggregator reads
true minimum costs, sweeps budgets 20, 50, 100, 200, and 300, and reports
targets for which stage-1 counting is unaffordable or unavailable and a
higher-stage move gives a path within budget
(`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration13/aggregate_annealing.py:1-29,84-155,267-299`).
The local analysis output is JSON with a frontier, a reorganization gradient,
a strategy census, and stage reachability counts
(`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration13/work/annealing_analysis.json:1-94`).

This search has directed, costed edges, but their source is the machine author.
The stage ladder is explicitly described as model-given rather than discovered
(`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration13/README.md:72-89`). It
therefore demonstrates the computation Hermes could run after obtaining
dependency data; it does not provide lesson or practice prerequisites.

## 3. The curriculum-linked Atlas

The later Atlas supplies a lesson-indexed input surface. Its cell generator
reads two predicates:
`compiled_lesson_task_instance/3` and `lesson_task_instance/3`. A compiled row
contains a lesson code, a productive or deformation role, a concrete task, and
source evidence naming the extraction rule and teacher-guide location
(`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration14/generate_cells.pl:23-26,55-66`;
`../umedcta-formalization/lessons/im/generated/compiled_task_instances.pl:1-18`).
The traversal contract keeps source-backed instances separate from operation
attachments and candidate deformations
(`../umedcta-formalization/learner/activity_contract.pl:1990-2028`).

This representation is a set of lesson-to-task facts, not a curriculum
precedence graph. The Atlas runs every task instance independently at each
configured stage. Each transition begins at
`learner_state(Stage, [])`, so strategy inventory and exposure do not carry
from an earlier lesson into a later lesson
(`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration14/export_atlas.pl:29-37,94-121,133-158`).
The output is JSONL with lesson, task, provenance, entering stage, successor
state, observation, crisis classification, and reorganization cost for that
single step
(`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration14/export_atlas.pl:173-215,263-285`).

The iteration-15 archive confirms this historical shape. Its Atlas used 151
lesson shards and three entering stages, produced 2,463 records, and stated
that every transition began with an empty inventory
(`../umedcta-formalization/umedcta_iter15_work.tar.gz::scripts/bigred/iteration15/work/atlas/cells.json:1`;
`../umedcta-formalization/umedcta_iter15_work.tar.gz::scripts/bigred/iteration15/work/atlas/atlas_summary.json:200-220`).
The same summary records 908 audited lessons without compiled events through
grade 6, which marks representation gaps rather than prerequisite failures
(`../umedcta-formalization/umedcta_iter15_work.tar.gz::scripts/bigred/iteration15/work/atlas/atlas_summary.json:166-202`).

## 4. Transfer to Hermes

### Steelman: what transfers

Once Hermes has a typed dependency graph, the iteration-13 shape transfers
cleanly:

- filter admissible edges by an entering ability profile or coordination stage;
- compute minimum-cost and witness paths under a declared budget;
- compare the reachable set before and after adding an ability;
- distinguish a cheaper route from a newly possible route;
- count alternative routes and expose bottlenecks or uncovered regions.

Those results would add information that the present Hermes graph does not
carry. Job 7771830 records lesson sequence, lesson-to-strategy,
strategy-elaboration, and crosswalk-family edges, but its declared scope
excludes learner simulation and path scoring
(`.superpowers/sdd/task-112-curriculum-graph-report.md:14-30`). A sound
precedence layer could turn those recorded associations into context for a
separate reachability computation while preserving the distinction between
sequence, association, and requirement.

The Atlas also contributes a useful seam. Its source-backed lesson-to-task rows
can support claims about what a lesson asks a represented learner to do, and
its single-step transitions can provide candidate costs or stage-conditioned
outcomes. Those are inputs to precedence adjudication, not precedence edges
themselves
(`../umedcta-formalization/lessons/im/generated/compiled_task_instances.pl:13-18`;
`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration14/export_atlas.pl:17-23,185-215`).

### Rebuttal: why it cannot be transferred as-is

Hermes currently has zero prerequisite edges. The baseline deliberately
infers none and warns that published order is curriculum sequence, not evidence
that a learner must traverse a unique path
(`.superpowers/sdd/task-112-curriculum-graph-report.md:21-32`). Treating the
existing lesson-sequence edges as prerequisites would change their meaning.
Treating lesson-to-strategy attachment as `requires` would likewise collapse an
association into a dependency.

The iteration-5 carpet cannot repair that absence because its axes are operand
values and its cells summarize authored template coverage. Iteration 13 cannot
repair it because its only edges are the authored unit moves. The Atlas cannot
repair it because it resets the learner state for every lesson-task-stage cell.
Running any of these algorithms over lesson order without a new edge contract
would make the desired precedence relation an assumption of the computation
rather than a result
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/CARVING_CLAIM.md:24-38`;
`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/units_machine.pl:46-69`;
`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration14/export_atlas.pl:29-37`).

The eventual structure should also be treated as a typed dependency DAG or
hypergraph, not assumed to be a tree. A lesson may require several abilities,
and an ability may have alternative sufficient routes. The iteration-5
multiple-template cells and the iteration-13 witness paths demonstrate why
preserving alternatives matters, although neither artifact establishes any
particular curriculum dependency
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/analyze.py:242-258,363-383`;
`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration13/export_annealing.pl:125-175`).

## 5. What must be authored or derived

A sound Hermes transfer requires the following new data contract.

| Required layer | Minimum content | Defensible source and status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Typed dependency edge | `requires(consumer, prerequisite, relation_kind, scope, provenance, status)` with a fixed direction | Explicit curriculum language or reviewed expert authorship. Any extraction is a candidate until adjudicated. |
| Lesson demand | `lesson_requires_ability(Lesson, Ability, Receipt)` | Source-backed task statements, monitoring evidence, or activity contracts. A lesson-to-strategy association is supporting evidence, not automatically a requirement. |
| Lesson opportunity | `lesson_occasions_ability(Lesson, Ability, Receipt)` | Source-backed activity evidence. Keep “requires” separate from “introduces,” “practices,” and “assesses.” |
| Ability dependency | `ability_requires(Ability, PriorAbility, Receipt)` or an AND/OR prerequisite set | Executable proof bodies and automata can generate candidates. Literature or expert review must decide whether a computational dependency is also a pedagogical prerequisite. |
| Learner state | Entering ability inventory, exposure history, and any coordination-stage attribution | Declared model input with receipts. The Atlas's empty inventory is suitable for isolated probes, not curriculum-prefix reachability. |
| Edge cost | A named cost unit and rule for combining costs | Authored or empirically estimated. Lesson index, graph hop count, and inference steps must not be silently treated as interchangeable. |
| Missing-data policy | `unknown`, `represented_absence`, `budget_exhausted`, and `excluded` kept distinct | Follow the paper's typed boundary discipline rather than interpreting every missing path as “not required” or “unreachable” (`../umedcta-formalization/docs/proposals/2026-07-10-curriculum-dynamics/curriculum-dynamics.tex:352-394`). |

With those layers, the heat-map method would give Hermes a matrix of
stage-conditioned feasible lessons or abilities and a count of represented
routes per cell. The iteration-13 method would add budget frontiers, minimum
costs, witnesses, and the marginal unlock from adding an ability. The current
lesson-sequence and lesson-strategy edges give ordering and association only;
they cannot answer those questions without the new dependency semantics
(`.superpowers/sdd/task-112-curriculum-graph-report.md:14-32`).

IMPLEMENTATION_COMPLETE

**Verdict: right-tree-wrong-branch.** The prior work establishes a useful
method for asking which represented targets remain feasible under an authored
ability gate and resource budget. That method can strengthen Hermes by adding
budget frontiers, witness paths, alternative-route counts, and typed failure
once lesson and ability dependencies have provenance. The existing heat-map
does not contain curriculum precedence, the unit machine contains only
model-authored arithmetic moves, and the Atlas evaluates isolated lesson tasks
from reset states. Hermes should keep the current sequence and strategy edges
as recorded relations, author or derive a separately adjudicated dependency
layer, and only then apply the reachability machinery
(`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/CARVING_CLAIM.md:24-38`;
`../umedcta-formalization/tools/carving/units_machine.pl:38-69`;
`../umedcta-formalization/scripts/bigred/iteration14/export_atlas.pl:29-37`;
`.superpowers/sdd/task-112-curriculum-graph-report.md:21-32`).
