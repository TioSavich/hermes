## Reader changes

The canonical reader now:

- parses parenthesized arithmetic recursively while retaining ordinary
  multiplication-before-addition precedence;
- expands an equation chain into one ordered claim per adjacent pair and keeps
  duplicate links, with `sequence_index` and `sequence_length` on each reading;
- emits `ratio_statement/1` and `proportion_statement/2` rather than
  `arithmetic_equation/2` for explicit ratio and proportion syntax;
- accepts the bounded interrupted result frames found in the red ledger,
  including `which would be` and `or just, which is`;
- retains explicit argument roles and token spans, normalized units, and
  inferred percent normalization as separate reading fields; and
- records bounded antecedent candidates separately from unresolved referents.
  A modal, report, or quotation frame changes the pragmatic fields but not the
  mathematical term.

Ratio and proportion are reader terms in this slice. The fenced checker has no
domain adjudicator for those functors, so it honestly returns `not_covered`
rather than treating either form as equality.

The regressions were added before the corresponding grammar changes. The first
parenthesis run failed by returning only the suffix
`arithmetic_equation(4,20)`. A second red regression showed that ordinary term
deduplication collapsed `7 = 7 = 7` to one link. Both failures are retained as
passing regressions in `scripts/checks/math_claim_language.pl`.

## Preservation and abstention audit

All ten previously read utterances remain read. Comparing the complete
pre-change checked records against the final records found zero missing or
changed claim records. The four added checked records are the surfaces listed
above, and the only silence-to-read changes are `u0270` and `u0377`.

The reader continues to abstain where a term would require supplied content:

- `I got 18 for that` has no explicit operand source for `that`;
- `u0337`, “We have 1/3 block left over, which is half a pizza,” requires a
  referent conversion and whole not fixed by the utterance;
- `u0416`, “27 divided by four, so that means ...,” omits the result; and
- a division expression such as `14 divided by 3/4` without a claimed result
  remains an expression rather than an equation.

For accepted `which` frames, the operands and result are explicit. The reading
records `which` as unresolved and places the preceding mathematical span in
`antecedent_candidates`; it does not use that candidate to rewrite the term.
